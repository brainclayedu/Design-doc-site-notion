Meaty

Goals

The Meaty mechanic is designed to:

  • Reward Precision Timing: Grant frame advantage by hitting opponents during their wakeup frames, encouraging skillful execution.
  • Enhance Tactical Depth: Provide pressure options following a knockdown.
  • Support Readability: Deliver clear feedback through the heads-up display and subtle visual reactions.

Overview

Meaty attacks, sometimes called "late hits" in casual terms or used as nouns (e.g., "meaty hit"), are strikes timed to connect with an opponent during their wakeup frames after a knockdown. By hitting later in an attack’s active frames, meaty attacks maximize frame advantage, pressuring recovering foes and turning a successful knockdown into a strategic opportunity. This mechanic, a staple of fighting game design, applies in The Fighting Game to any wakeup scenario—whether from a soft knockdown’s automatic recovery or a hard knockdown’s prone state recovery, as detailed in "Knockdown to Prone." Opponents can counter meaty attacks spatially through recovery options like rolling to crouch. In group combat, meaty attacks can hit multiple foes, though only those struck during wakeup frames qualify as "meaty." Success is signaled by a "MEATY" notification on the heads-up display and a subtle visual reaction from the opponent, ensuring clarity amidst the high-fantasy combat chaos.

Key Features

  • Wakeup Pressure: Meaty attacks target opponents during wakeup from soft knockdowns (quick auto-recovery) or prone recoveries (variable back or front orientations), with frame advantage scaling based on how late the attack’s active frames connect—later hits yield greater advantage.
  • Construction Variability: Dependent on the custom skill system, meaty potential varies by attack design—e.g., a directional-state strike with extended active frames suits close-range pressure, while an aimed-state projectile with delayed impact excels at spacing.
  • Multi-Foe Impact: In group combat, attacks strike all foes within range, but only those hit during wakeup frames are considered "meaty," maintaining the mechanic’s situational focus.
  • Feedback Signals: A "MEATY" notification appears on the heads-up display upon successful timing, paired with a subtle visual reaction from the opponent (e.g., an extended hit reaction), enhancing readability without overt effects.

Meaty Attack Spectrum

  • First Frame Meaty Attacks: These hit immediately as the opponent becomes vulnerable on wakeup, often blending with pressure tactics like jump-ins, though distinct from traditional safe jumps to prioritize player commitment over option selects.
  • Degrees of Meatiness: Meaty exists on a spectrum—attacks hitting earlier in their active frames are "slightly meaty," while those landing on the latest frames are "very meaty," with no formal tiers; players use these informal descriptors for precision discussions.

Sliding Hitbox Meaty

  • Some meaty attacks exploit sliding hitboxes—forward-moving strikes or momentum from sprinting—to delay impact, aligning active frames with wakeup. This leverages the opponent’s brief invincibility during wakeup animations, ensuring the hit connects as vulnerability resumes.

Meaty Attack Timeline

  • Compared to a normal attack, a meaty attack hits later despite starting simultaneously, extending the defender’s recovery relative to the attacker. Blockstun duration remains identical, but the delayed hit can shift an attack from negative on block (e.g., -2 frames) to positive (e.g., +3 frames), sometimes allowing the attacker to recover in time to block reversals.

Moves to Use or Avoid

  • Optimal meaty attacks feature high blockstun and low recovery frames to maximize frame advantage, yet must activate quickly enough to catch the opponent as they become vulnerable. Slow-startup moves risk missing the wakeup window, while fast, low-stun attacks may not capitalize on the advantage fully.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Prone Recovery Evasion: Recovery options from prone (e.g., rolling to crouch) may position the opponent outside meaty range, reducing reliability.
    • Solution: Emphasize spatial counterplay—successful meaty attacks require predicting recovery movement, leveraging strafing or dashing from the "Movement" system to cover escape paths.
  • Reversal Tension: Meaty frame advantage might overlap with reversal invincibility windows (per "Reversal"), limiting defender options if recovery is too safe.
    • Solution: Balance via prototyping—ensure meaty recovery frames allow reversals to punish overextensions (e.g., a +4 meaty loses to a 3-frame invincible Special), preserving the counter-system dynamic.
  • 3D Readability: Precise active-frame timing may be less intuitive in 3D animations compared to 2D sprite-based fighters.
    • Solution: Use the heads-up display "MEATY" notification and extended hit reaction visuals (e.g., a prolonged hit pose) for clarity; test animation readability in prototyping to avoid reliance on overt effects like slow motion, aligning with cinematic goals.

Suggestions for Prototyping

  • RPG Integration: Test tying meaty success to resource systems—e.g., restoring 5% Energy or adding a portion of a Poise block per hit—to enhance tactical rewards, assessing impact on resource balance.
  • Defender Option Selects: Explore recovery moves that counter meaty attacks (e.g., a roll beating strikes but losing to grabs), favoring defenders to offset offensive pressure; evaluate how this affects the commitment-focused design.

Conclusion

Meaty attacks blend fighting game precision with The Fighting Game’s high-fantasy combat, delivering frame advantage through expertly timed wakeup pressure. Applicable across soft and hard knockdowns, they adapt to custom-crafted skill designs, offering variety via construction while naturally scaling to hit multiple foes in group scenarios. With heads-up display feedback and subtle visual reactions ensuring clarity, meaty attacks enrich the "Core Loop"’s prediction and advantage phases, rewarding skill without overshadowing reversal counterplay. Prototyping will refine balance, readability, and potential RPG ties, solidifying meaty as a cornerstone of strategic depth.
