Grabs

Goals

The Grab mechanic is designed to:

  • Reward Positional and Timing Precision: Deliver unblockable, proximity-based attacks requiring exact range and state awareness, encouraging skillful execution.
  • Enhance Tactical Depth: Offer varied grab types—normal, charged, special, and control—with distinct roles from pressure to repositioning and combo initiation.
  • Support Readability: Provide clear heads-up display notifications and subtle visual reactions for diverse grab attempts in a 3D, high-fantasy environment.
  • Integrate RPG Elements: Tie grabs to resource systems (e.g., Poise, Energy) and state transitions, blending fighting game precision with RPG strategy.

Overview

Grabs are proximity-based attacks that opponents cannot guard against, succeeding only if the defender lacks grab invulnerability and falls within range. Non-charged grabs (normal/command) are directional, while charged grabs may shift to aimed inputs via the Focus state, pending prototyping—each type balances speed, range, and power. Successful grabs place the attacker in a grappling state and the defender in a grappled state for a brief window (3 seconds minimum), enabling repositioning or follow-ups from a state-based moveset (see "Grappling State Skills"), unless broken by inaction, a button-mash escape (10F fill-meter minigame), or countered during startup. Normal grabs span proximity, standard, air, and prone variants; charged and special grabs escalate with invincibility and unescapable throws; hit grabs mimic aspects of grabs as cinematic strikes; and control skills from the Impact state apply status effects via Poise. Grab invulnerability (+5F post-stun) prevents loops, while priority rules (strikes > grabs > guard) and grab clashes shape counterplay. Single-target by design, grabs integrate with custom skill designs, with success or clashes marked by "GRAB" or "CLASH" on the heads-up display and subtle visual reactions, ensuring clarity in The Fighting Game’s dynamic chaos.

Key Features

  • Conditions for Success: Grabs connect if the defender lacks grab invulnerability (e.g., not in blockstun +5F) and is within range—directional for normal/command, potentially aimed for charged.
  • Normal Grabs:
    • Proximity Grabs: Very close range, instant startup (~0F), Counter Hit state on whiff—punish pressure or break blocks.
    • Normal/Command Grabs: Longer range, moderate startup (~3-5F, Grab + direction), offensively pierce blocks or defensively challenge pressure; no Counter Hit on whiff.
    • Air Grabs: Target foes slightly above when rising, used as anti-air or air-to-air; 10-frame punishable landing recovery on whiff (no Counter Hit).
    • Prone Grabs: Lift from prone or ground grapple into submissions, extending post-knockdown pressure.
  • Charged Grabs: Held input (~15F startup), unescapable, longer duration/range, grab invincibility—beats normal grabs, whiffs vs. other charged (longer active frames win).
  • Special Grabs: Held + motion input (~15-20F startup), unescapable, flow into throws for damage, higher range/damage, grab invincibility—beats normal/charged, whiffs vs. other specials (longer active frames win).
  • Hit Grabs: Strikes with cinematic follow-ups (not true grabs), countered by deflect shields—see "Attack Types" for expansion.
  • Grab Invulnerability: Hitbox-specific invulnerability (per "Invincible") during prejump, blockstun/hitstun/knockdown +5F, or moves with grab/full invulnerability (e.g., Supers, Burst); some ground attacks count as airborne.
  • Priority Rules: Strikes > Grabs > Guard; Normal vs. Normal = Grab Clash; Normal vs. Charged = Charged wins; Charged vs. Charged = Whiff (active frames decide); Charged vs. Special = Special wins; Special vs. Special = Whiff (active frames decide). Stronger grabs (higher point value in skill creation) cost more but extend active frames for priority, per future skill system design.
  • Grab Clash: Normal grab input within 10F of being grabbed cancels both into a 31F invincible animation (restores air options in air); unavailable in Faultless Defense, Counter Hit state, or recovery.
  • Control Skill: Impact state grab (press/hold), costs Poise to initiate grappling state or directly apply effects: Root, Stun, Confuse, Polymorph, Daze, Disarm, Blind, Slow, Push/Knockback, Pull.
  • Feedback Signals: "GRAB" (success) or "CLASH" (clash) on heads-up display, with visual reactions (e.g., quick reach for normal, glowing grip for charged), enhancing readability.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Counterplay Balance: Slower startup grabs (normal/charged/special) risk strike interrupts, weakening unblockability.
    • Solution: Retain strikes > grabs priority; proximity grabs’ instant startup preserves pressure, refined in prototyping.
  • Whiff Vulnerability: Proximity grabs’ Counter Hit state and air grabs’ landing recovery expose aggressors.
    • Solution: Tune recovery (e.g., 20-30F ground, 10F air) in prototyping to balance risk/reward, per "Core Loop" prediction.
  • 3D Readability: Varied grab startups and air/prone positioning may blur in 3D animations.
    • Solution: Use HUD tags ("GRAB"/"CLASH") and subtle tells (e.g., glowing hand for charged), testing visibility in 3rd-person view.
  • Grab Clash Restrictions: No clashes in Counter Hit/Faultless Defense might over-penalize proximity grabs.
    • Solution: Keep as GG Strive-inspired design, prototyping to ensure mash counterplay doesn’t dominate.

Suggestions for Prototyping

  • Aimed Charged Grabs: Test charged grabs as aimed inputs via Focus state, assessing 3D positioning (e.g., air grabs) and startup impact.
  • RPG Integration: Test Energy regen boost (~5% per second, max ~15% over 3 seconds) during grappling state, with Poise costs for charged/special grabs, balancing resource interplay.
  • Escapability: Evaluate if charged/special grabs’ unescapable status holds, or if a late break window (e.g., 20F) adds depth.
  • Multi-Target Potential: Explore dual-hand grabs (2 foes max) with "Grappling State Skills" follow-ups for multi-foe effects, ensuring single-target focus remains viable.

Conclusion

Grabs fuse fighting game precision with The Fighting Game’s RPG depth, offering unblockable pressure through proximity, charged, special, and control variants. From instant proximity grabs to invincible special throws and Poise-fueled control skills, they initiate grappling states for repositioning or attacks, adapting to custom designs while staying single-target. With heads-up display feedback and subtle visuals ensuring clarity, grabs enhance the "Core Loop"’s prediction and advantage phases, complementing meaty setups and reversals within invulnerability bounds. Prototyping will refine startup, readability, and RPG ties, cementing grabs as a versatile, strategic tool in this high-fantasy arena.
