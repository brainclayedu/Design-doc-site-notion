Deflect Shield

Deflect Shield in The Fighting Game is a Burst-powered parry, a clutch counter to incoming strikes and projectiles in its high-fantasy, 3rd-person combat arena. Activated from block or blockstun with a 50% Burst Gauge cost, it deflects all attacks within its window—pushing foes back based on proximity—offering players a deft tool to disrupt aggression and seize control. Its tight timing rewards precision, balanced by resource use and tuned by build customization for tactical flair.

Goals

The Deflect Shield system strives to:

  • Counter Threats: Neutralize multiple incoming attacks—melee or ranged—broadening defense beyond evasion or static guards.
  • Demand Precision: Require exact timing from block states, aligning with the game’s skill-driven combat focus.
  • Leverage Burst: Draw from the Burst Gauge for a potent counter, pacing its use within 2-3 fight instances.
  • Enhance Build Play: Adjust timing, pushback, and resilience via stats, gear, and skills, reflecting player style.

Overview

Triggered by Block + Heavy Attack (held briefly/longpress) in block or blockstun states, Deflect Shield consumes 50% of the Burst Gauge to parry all attacks within a narrow window—neutralizing them with a pushback that scales by foe proximity. Similar to Wild Assault (an Impact state special), it uses the Guard state as a base for its Guard state Heavy attack special (short charge) chorded input, carving a defensive niche from block contexts.

Core Mechanics

Activation and Cost

Deflect Shield demands sharp timing:

  • Mechanics:
    • Input: Block + Heavy Attack (held/longpress)—e.g., LB + RT hold—from block or blockstun—e.g., post-Block of a Medium Attack.
    • Cost: Drains 50% Burst Gauge—e.g., half a bar—limiting use to 2-4 times per fight with regen.
  • Combat Role: Counters all attacks in its window—e.g., deflecting a flurry of strikes or projectiles—disrupting multi-foe pressure.
  • Tactical Use: Turns aggression into risk—e.g., deflecting a combo with pushback—balanced by cost and precision.

Deflection and Pushback

The shield deflects with proximity-based force:

  • Mechanics:
    • Deflection: Negates all incoming attacks—e.g., melee strikes or spells—if timed right, akin to a multi-hit parry.
    • Pushback: Scales with foe closeness—e.g., close melee foes shoved far, distant ranged are pushed somewhat less—tuned by gear, skills, and build.
  • Combat Role: Stops damage—e.g., nullifying a ranged Special—or disrupts—e.g., pushing close foes out of range—offering a counter window.
  • Tactical Use: Punishes tight aggression—e.g., deflecting a multi-foe rush—or stalls—e.g., nudging a distant caster—offset by Burst cost and timing.

Challenges and Solutions

Deflect Shield poses hurdles:

  • Timing Precision: Narrow window might frustrate—e.g., missing a deflect feels punishing.
    • Solution: Widen slightly—e.g., a forgiving frame buffer—or scale with agility—e.g., rogues get more leeway—balancing skill and access.
  • Pushback Clarity: Proximity-based pushback might confuse—e.g., why less push on ranged foes?
    • Solution: Cue visually—e.g., glow scales with distance—or clarify—e.g., melee focus in UI—enhancing readability.
  • Burst Cost vs. Utility: 50% cost might underwhelm—e.g., Psych Burst’s reset overshadows.
    • Solution: Add minor perk—e.g., multi-deflect staggers briefly—or tweak cost—e.g., 40%—balancing with Psych’s full-bar reset.
