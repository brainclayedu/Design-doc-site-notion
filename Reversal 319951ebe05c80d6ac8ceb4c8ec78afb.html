Reversal

Goals

The Reversal system aims to:

  • Reward Precise Timing: Enable frame-perfect counters post-recovery to shift momentum.
  • Enhance Tactical Depth: Offer invincible or high-priority moves to challenge pressure.
  • Support Feedback: Use HUD cues to confirm successful execution for learning.
  • Integrate with Resources: Balance availability through Energy and Poise costs.

Overview

Reversal in The Fighting Game refers to actions executed on the first actionable frame after recovery from blockstun, hitstun, or knockdown, often using Special Attacks (10-20% Energy) or Super Attacks (3-5 Poise blocks) to counter opponent pressure with invincibility or priority. The HUD flashes "REVERSAL" when a Special or Super lands on this frame, signaling perfect timing. These moves—commonly invincible on startup—excel at flipping disadvantage into opportunity, with options varying by character build and state (e.g., Impact State knockups).

Core Reversal System

Reversal operates as follows:

  • Mechanics: Triggers on the first frame post-recovery—blockstun, hitstun, or knockdown—using Specials (10-20% Energy) or Supers (3-5 Poise blocks); Specials offer quick, targeted counters (e.g., Impact knockups), while Supers deliver high-damage, invincible reversals (e.g., super freeze); invincibility varies—some Specials have hitbox protection, Supers often power through; HUD shows "REVERSAL" on success, aiding timing mastery; prototyping will refine invincibility duration (e.g., 5-10 frames).
  • States: Usable in Base (directional Specials), Impact (aimed knockups/control), or Guard (defensive setup); air reversals possible post-jump recovery—e.g., an air Special counters aerial pressure.
  • Types: Specials (e.g., Wild Assault, Control Skills) for low-cost counters; Supers for high-impact turnarounds—character builds dictate options (e.g., agile builds favor Specials, tanks lean on Supers).

Integration with Combat

Reversal ties into key systems:

  • Poise Gauge: Supers drain 3-5 Poise blocks, regenerating toward 2.5 via hits, blocks, or parries—Poise Break at zero risks overuse, cracking defenses.
  • Energy: Specials sap 10-20% Energy (shield layer per "Gauges"), balancing frequency with health exposure.
  • Impact State: Enhances reversals with aimed knockups or control skills, amplifying options post-recovery.
  • Counter Attack: Reversals landing pre-recovery may register as Counter Hits (overriding "REVERSAL" with "COUNTER"), boosting damage.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Timing Precision: Frame-perfect execution may frustrate novices.

    Solution: Widen input buffer (e.g., 3-5 frames) for "REVERSAL" HUD, easing learning—prototyping will test accessibility vs. skill ceiling.

  • Overuse Risk: Frequent invincible reversals could stall pacing.

    Solution: Tie Supers to 3-5 Poise blocks, Specials to Energy—Poise Break adds vulnerability, balanced via prototyping (e.g., 2-3 uses per exchange).

  • State Clarity: Impact vs. Base reversals might blur roles.

    Solution: Limit invincibility to Supers, Specials use hitbox protection—prototyping will clarify state distinctions.

Conclusions

Reversal empowers players to seize control on recovery’s first frame, blending Specials (10-20% Energy) and Supers (3-5 Poise blocks) with invincibility or priority to counter pressure. Integrated with "Poise Gauge," "Energy," "Impact State," and "Counter Attack," it rewards timing and resource management, scaling to group combat via build-specific options. Prototyping will fine-tune invincibility, buffers, and costs, ensuring Reversals enhance the core loop’s strategic flow with thrilling turnarounds.
