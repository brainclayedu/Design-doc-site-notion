Inspirational Games & References

Inspirational Games & ReferencesOverviewThis page collects the key games and design references that have shaped the vision for The Fighting Game and its future expansions. Each reference is tied directly to specific mechanics in the core design document so we can clearly see how we are learning from the best examples while creating something distinct.

Bungie’s ONI (2001)

  • Melee combat that is controlled, robust and contextual. Attacks can hit multiple targets and allows the player to manage multiple opponents well.
  • Environmental Transparency: Walls and objects between camera and player become semi-transparent. This is a core solution we are adopting to maintain visibility in 3rd-person.
  • One of the original inspirations for the combat in this game — it proved 3rd-person melee can feel readable and fair with simple transparency tricks and contextual multi-opponent handling.

Guilty Gear Strive

  • Modern Yomi & Readability: Strong rock-paper-scissors layers, clear frame data in training mode, and accessible onboarding. Influences our Core Loop, training tools, and matchmaking expectations.
  • Core fighting game elements and mechanics inspiration.
  • Roman Cancels and advanced cancel system inspired our cancel mechanics; strong emphasis on readable animations and Yomi layers shaped our Core Loop and defines the core balancing mechanism for the game.

Helldivers

  • Cinematic dynamic action and environmental destruction.
  • Chaotic friendly fire and systemic elements.
  • Unpredictable chaos tempered by accessible mechanics; inspires our cinematic physical chaos, environmental interactivity in arenas, and the friendly-fire risk that makes group combat feel dangerous and strategic.

Shadowbane

  • Early MMORPG combat with player-driven builds and large-scale PvP feel.
  • Broad RPG character/class building depth with classes, stats, and disciplines.
  • Player built cities and siege mechanics.
  • Shows how combat systems can scale from small fights to massive battles; informs our long-term MMORPG vision for skill customization and group combat.

Def Jam Fight for New York

  • Grappling & Environmental Interaction: Wrestling-style grabs and environmental throws.
  • Fighting game mechanics.
  • Combat-centric environmental interaction.
  • Combat pacing.
  • Character skill customization through moveset combinations.
  • Directly inspired our Grappling State Skills, prone/grappling mechanics, and how we want physical chaos to feel satisfying in 3rd-person; shows how grabs can lead to meaningful follow-ups, environmental use, and robust character customization through moveset combinations.

WWE No Mercy (N64)

  • Expands on Def Jam Fight for New York's character skill customization, allowing very robust custom wrestlers.
  • Expands combat-centric environmental interaction, adding ropes, cages etc.
  • Demonstrates how deep customization and interactive environments can create highly personal playstyles; reinforces our modular skill system and shows how arenas should feel alive and reactive during grappling and throws.

Street Fighter 6

  • Modern Yomi & Readability: Strong rock-paper-scissors layers, clear frame data in training mode, and accessible onboarding.
  • Influences our Core Loop, training tools, and matchmaking expectations; modern accessibility standards we want to match, especially for onboarding new players to our 3rd-person control scheme.

Assassin's Creed Valhalla

  • Combat controls (bumper and trigger usage for attacks).
  • Traversal movement parkour.
  • Shows how state-based inputs can feel intuitive in 3rd-person; informs our control mappings and state transitions (Base/Impact/Guard), making the large number of options feel approachable.

Wildstar

  • Attack telegraphs and clear visual feedback.
  • Smooth movement.
  • Group based PvP.
  • Strong emphasis on readable telegraphs in action combat; helps us ensure attacks feel fair and predictable in 3rd-person, especially for reach and hit/hurtbox communication.

Blade & Soul

  • Fluid combo chaining and stance-based combat.
  • Contextual combat skills.
  • Influences our cancel system and state-based movement; shows how stance changes can create deep combat expression and tie into customization for different playstyles.

Black Desert Online

  • Action-Oriented MMORPG Combat: Combo chains, skill customization, and large-scale PvP feel.
  • Influences how our combat scales to larger worlds and player-driven builds in the MMORPG layer; shows how skill customization can define space and control in group fights.

Diablo 4

  • The player has to deal with many enemies but often they are easy to dispatch.
  • Influences how the gameplay may feel versus minion-centric players; strong influence for MOBA and MMORPG gameplay where crowd control and area management become key.

Crimson Desert

  • High-fantasy action combat with environmental interaction.
  • Robust movesets.
  • Cinematic flair and world integration; inspires our high-fantasy aesthetic and environmental hazards, helping us make arenas feel alive and impactful.

Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice

  • Posture & Stagger System: Clear feedback on enemy state and the satisfaction of breaking posture. This directly informs our CC Meter buildup and Poise mechanics.
  • Precision & Intent: Lock-on and camera recentering that make attacks feel exactly where the player aims. Reinforces our goal of strong intent interpretation and clear state feedback.

God of War 2018 & Ragnarök

  • Camera & Intent Assist: The over-the-shoulder camera and intelligent attack targeting that blends player aim with soft auto-target cones. This directly informs our 3rd-person camera behavior and intent assist system to ensure attacks feel like they go where the player intends, even in chaotic fights.
  • Cinematic Physicality: Dynamic, weighty combat with environmental interaction and satisfying stagger reactions. This inspires our emphasis on cinematic physical chaos and environmental hazards in arenas.
  • Readability Solutions: Clear visual feedback (sparks, posture cues) and directional indicators for off-screen threats. We are adopting similar non-intrusive cues and environmental transparency.

Your Only Move is Hustle

  • Resource-Driven Combo Chaining: Tight resource management and combo building in a turn-based format. This directly inspired our resource tension and how we want players to chain actions within the Yomi loop.

Path of Exile 2

  • Deep Skill Customization: Modular skill gems and support mods that dramatically change playstyle. This is the primary reference for our modular skill system and how customization lets players define the space they control.

Batman: Arkham Series

  • Counter & Flow: Clear enemy intent prompts and satisfying counter system. Informs our Guard State and reversal mechanics.

Scaling ContextThese references are not just for the fighting game core — they also inform how mechanics will evolve:

  • Path of Exile 2 and customization depth → MMORPG class trees and player-driven builds
  • Shadowbane and Black Desert → large-scale combat feel in MOBA/MMORPG layers
