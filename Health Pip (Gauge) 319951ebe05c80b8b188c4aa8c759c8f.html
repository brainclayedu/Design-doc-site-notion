Health Pip (Gauge)

The Health Pip Gauge in The Fighting Game stands as the final measure of a character’s vitality, tracking their survival through a brutal high-fantasy, 3rd-person combat world. Represented as a row of discrete pips—each a fragile unit of life—it captures the tension of dwindling resilience, protected by the Energy Gauge until its shield falters. Featuring Current Health Pips, Shadow Health, Gray Health, and Empty Health Pips, this system balances recoverable and permanent damage, ensuring every hit resonates with consequence and every recovery demands strategy.

Goals

The Health Pip Gauge is crafted to:

  • Measure Vitality: Provide a segmented, tangible gauge of life, where each pip’s loss heightens stakes.
  • Sync with Energy: Rely on Energy protection until a critical threshold, then expose Health to direct damage.
  • Offer Recovery Paths: Enable players to reclaim lost health through universal mechanics, adding tactical depth.
  • Reflect Build Diversity: Adjust pip count and resilience via stats, gear, and skills, enhancing RPG customization.

Overview

The Health Pip Gauge depletes when the Energy Gauge falls below its breakpoint or when certain effects bypass Energy protection—each pip a full unit lost to attacks, with damage over time (DoTs) like bleeding or burning eroding fractions. It includes:

  • Current Health Pips: Active vitality units.
  • Shadow Health: Recent damage that slowly decays unless recovered by actions.
  • Gray Health: Lost portions that can regenerate over time or via skills.
  • Empty Health Pips: Permanently lost vitality, unrecoverable in-fight.

The final pip resists depletion—requiring significant force like a Special or Super—to trigger a downed state, a fleeting chance to rise or fall. Armor mitigates damage to both Energy and Health, while hurtbox-specific attacks (mid, low, high) or armor weak points modify impact, tying health to combat precision.

Core Mechanics

Health Pip

Health pips represent blocks of health that can be depleted in whole by taking unguarded hits:

  • Mechanics:
    • Health Pool: The Total health value is calculated as a pool, a value that can be diminsed in part via chip damage, damage over time, and other damage that may bypass the energy health breakpoint.
    • Partial Bypass: Effects like bleeding, poison or burning partially bypass Energy—e.g., a bleed ticks a pip fraction even with Energy this can be modifed by armor mitigation.
    • Health Pip’s: The health Pool is broken into pips that can be depleted in whole pips if the energy breakpoint has been surpassed.
    • Pips and Armour: Armor can increase pip count and health pool amounts providing additional resilience to different types of damage.
  • Combat Role: Health pips determine how many hits (and at what strength) you can survive once your breakpoint has been reached on your energy gauge.
  • Tactical Use: Creates an urgency when your energy breakpoint has been reached as health can be exposed and rescued quickly once this happens.

Health Pip Depletion

Health pips erode when Energy protection fails or specific damage slips through:

  • Mechanics:
    • Energy Breakpoint: Below the breakpoint (Energy Gauge), attacks can remove full pips—e.g., a Heavy Attack takes a full pip, a Super or super could take more—Stats can boost pip count and or the total health pool value.
    • Armor Modifiers: Could protect health pip loss over the regions they occupy (Mid, Low, High)
  • Combat Role: Avoiding your health from depleting to zero and reduscing the opponent's health to zero is the objective of a match and leads directly to a win or defeat condition.
  • Tactical Use: Urges Energy maintenance—e.g., dodging to preserve pips—or risks bypass damage—e.g., bleeding ignores some Energy.

Shadow, Gray, and Empty Health

These universal states track damage aftermath:

  • Mechanics:
    • Shadow Health: Fresh attack damage leaves partial shadow health, which slowly decays to match current pips unless recovered by actions—e.g., hitting foes refills a portion—This is a form of recoverable “missing” health that can be regenerated for a brief window after it is lost.
    • Gray Health: Lost pips from damage can also leave gray health, recoverable over time passively or via skills—e.g., a special attack could regenerate a portion of gray health— this is a form of recoverable “missing” health that can remain available to regenerate unless the player is defeated.
    • Empty Health Pips: Fully depleted pips turn empty, unrecoverable in-fight.
  • Combat Role: Allows recovery—e.g., an effective counterattack refills some recently lost health by recovering shadow health—or a specific skill restores missing —e.g., gray regenerates slowly over time—while empty pips signal attrition.
  • Tactical Use: Promotes aggression to recover shadow health before it fades away, or the use of specific skills to recover Gray health or disengaging to attempt to recover. This could result in being too predictable or create opportunities to bait opponents.

Passive Health Regen

Health lost as Gray health regenerates over time passively to mitigate some damage types.

  • Mechanics:
    • Gray Health: Left behind from Damage, especially common with Damage over time, or damage that bypasses energy before it has reached it breakpoint.
    • Passive Regeneration: Greay Health will regenerate to Health over time based on gear, skills and buffs. This can outpace Damage over time in some cases.
  • Combat Role: Creates more reason to retreat and attempt to recover, helps mitigate aspects of Damage over time.
  • Tactical Use: Recover some health on retreat..

The Last Pip

The Final Pip of health is more resilient than the rest. While it can be overwhelmed by Damage over time that is heavier than the regeneration, breaking through the final pip requires a significantly slow attack, such as an impact, heavy special or super to break through the Pip and down the opponent.

  • Mechanics: The last pip resists lighter damage forcing slower stronger attacks to act as the killing blow. Attacks that break multiple pips can break through the final pip if the remaining pips are less or equal to what it would normally deplete.
  • Combat Role: Offers a final possibility to turn the tides.
  • Tactical Use: .

Downed State

Depleting the final pip triggers a downed state, a fragile lifeline:

  • Mechanics: The last pip resists loss—e.g., only stronger attacks break it—entering a state where Poise and Energy regen to rise or face a finishing blow.
  • Combat Role: Offers an opportunity to be saved in group combat.
  • Tactical Use: likely none for now, at least in 1v1 it is unlikely that a player would not finish of a downed opponent, this feature is more to lay the ground work for group combat and reviving downed allies.

Integration with Game Systems

The Health Pip Gauge ties into core mechanics:

  • Energy: Protects health until the breakpoint—e.g., a block drains Energy, sparing pips (Energy Gauge)—below which pips take full hits; bypass effects like bleeding erode both.
  • Combat: Normals take one pip—e.g., a Light Attack—Specials or Supers more—e.g., a Super cuts two (Combat sections)—DoTs bypass partially—e.g., burn ticks despite Energy.
  • Builds:
    • Fortitude: Increases pip count—e.g., tanks boast 7-9 vs. 5-6 base.
    • Agility: Boosts evasion, preserving pips—e.g., rogues dodge mid-hits.
    • Gear: Armor reduces pip loss—e.g., heavy plate cuts a Heavy Attack’s impact—light gear leans on mobility.
  • Movement: Actions like sprinting (Sprint/Traversal) drain Energy, risking pip exposure—e.g., over-sprinting depletes the shield (Energy Gauge).

Challenges and Solutions

Health pips present hurdles:

  • Depletion Clarity: Full-pip loss below breakpoint might confuse—e.g., when Energy fails mid-combo.
    • Solution: Flash pips below breakpoint—e.g., a red pulse—via UI cues for clarity.
  • Downed State Balance: Regen vs. vulnerability could skew—e.g., too safe or harsh.
    • Solution: Set a timed window—e.g., 5 seconds—to rise, simplifying stakes without extra resources.
  • Bypass Ambiguity: Partial bypass (e.g., bleeding) might muddle—e.g., when does Energy fail?
    • Solution: Define bypass triggers—e.g., DoTs only—and scale with Energy level—e.g., higher Energy cuts bleed damage.

Additional Context

Arenas shape health—hazards (e.g., lava) tax pips directly, cover aids gray regen—while class skills tweak recovery—warriors endure more pips, rogues regen gray faster—but shadow and gray remain universal damage states. Energy’s protective role (Energy Gauge) and movement costs (Mobility Skills) tie health to pacing, amplifying combat tension.

Conclusion

The Health Pip Gauge in The Fighting Game beats as the fragile core of survival, shielded by Energy until the breakpoint cracks. With current pips enduring hits, shadow and gray offering universal recovery from damage taken, and empty pips marking scars, it ensures every strike cuts deep—pushing players to wield Energy and armor to fend off the inevitable, in a relentless dance of resilience and resolve.
