Energy Gauge

The Energy Gauge in The Fighting Game fuels the pulse of action and resilience, a versatile resource that powers every strike, dash, and sprint while shielding Health in its high-fantasy, 3rd-person combat world. Designed as a natural mechanic—reflecting real-life stamina and the effort to mitigate damage—it drives the game’s tactical rhythm, rewarding precision and punishing exhaustion. Displayed as a bar with a midpoint breakpoint, Energy balances exertion and survival, shaped by build-specific traits and combat flow.

Goals

The Energy Gauge system is crafted to:

  • Unify Resource Use: Consolidate all actions—combat, movement, defense—into a single, intuitive pool, simplifying management with a natural feel.
  • Shield Health: Absorb damage to delay Health loss, mirroring energy’s role in deflecting or enduring blows.
  • Reward Skill: Refund Energy on successful combo hits, encouraging varied, well-timed attacks over repetition.
  • Reflect Customization: Adjust pool size, costs, and regeneration to suit playstyles—e.g., efficient tanks vs. expansive mages—with balanced tradeoffs.

Overview

The Energy Gauge powers every action—light jabs, dashes, sprints, and specials—while acting as a shield that depletes before Health takes lasting harm, akin to using stamina to position armor or dodge blows until exhaustion sets in. Energy drains with use—light moves sip, heavy attacks draw modestly, sprints steadily drain, and specials gulp—regenerating when idle, with a brief pause after hits. Successful hits (not blocked) refund Energy, favoring varied combos over repeats, with early hits refunding more—a cap limits excess, excluding specials and supers from bonuses. Pool sizes vary: tanks wield smaller pools with cheaper moves, mages larger ones with costlier casts—armor reduces damage to both, balancing depletion.

Core Mechanics

Energy as Fuel

Energy powers exertion, embodying the effort of combat and movement:

  • Mechanics: Costs scale naturally—light attacks (e.g., Light Attack from Controls) sip Energy, heavy attacks (e.g., Heavy Attack) sit between a sip and gulp, sprints (Sprint/Traversal) steadily drain (less out of combat), and specials or supers (Combat sections) gulp heavily. Costs adjust by build—tanks pay less for normals, mages more for spells—while agility speeds regen and heavy gear slows it. Crouching (Grounded Movement) costs no Energy but can trigger stealth skills with costs (Stealth).
  • Combat Role: Fuels every move—e.g., a Dash sips Energy, a Sprint steadily drains—driving combat and movement pacing.
  • Tactical Use: Encourages mixing light, efficient moves with heavier spends—e.g., a tank chains cheap jabs, a mage risks a gulp-worthy spell—offset by depletion risks.

Energy as Health Shield

Energy absorbs damage before Health, reflecting stamina’s role in reducing bodily harm:

  • Mechanics: Takes hits first—e.g., a Heavy Attack drains Energy—stopping at the midpoint breakpoint unless depleted, exposing Health. Armor reduces Energy and Health damage—e.g., a plated warrior loses less per hit—grey Energy tracks temporary loss, erased by hits.
  • Combat Role: Shields Health—e.g., a combo saps Energy, not pips—until the breakpoint, after which Health takes the brunt (Health Pip).
  • Tactical Use: Urges maintaining Energy—e.g., dodging to keep the shield—or risks permanent damage when exhausted—e.g., a stab hits Health directly.

Regeneration and Refunds

Energy replenishes and rewards naturally:

  • Regeneration: Restores quickly when idle—faster for agile builds—pausing briefly after damage (e.g., a short delay post-hit), resuming below the breakpoint to ensure action viability.
  • Refunds: Successful hits (not blocked) refund Energy—normals refund more than specials/supers (e.g., a jab refunds a decent chunk, a Super none), with early hits refunding more, late hits less. Combo variety boosts refunds—repeating attacks decays the refund (e.g., back-to-back jabs drop off fast), varied hits increase it (e.g., jab-to-slash gains more)—capped after a set hit count, excluding specials/supers from bonuses.
  • Tactical Use: Rewards precision—e.g., varied combos sustain a rogue—or pacing—e.g., pausing to regen—balanced by combo decay and cap.

Build-Specific Pool Sizes

Energy pool size reflects effort capacity:

  • Mechanics: Tanks have smaller pools (e.g., suited for fewer, cheaper moves), mages larger pools (e.g., for costlier spells)—agility boosts regen, strength cuts costs slightly. Armor reduces damage—hybrid builds (e.g., armored mages) balance pool size with armor tradeoffs—e.g., heavier gear might shrink the pool but lessen damage taken.
  • Combat Role: Tanks conserve with low-cost normals—e.g., a Heavy Attack costs less—mages burn through for big spells—e.g., a Special costs more.
  • Tactical Use: Lets tanks endure with efficiency—e.g., chaining refunded hits—while mages wield power—e.g., a spell barrage—balanced by regen and armor effects.

Integration with Game Systems

The Energy Gauge syncs with core mechanics:

  • Movement: Powers dashes, sprints, or climbs—e.g., a Dash sips Energy, a Sprint steadily drains (less out of combat)—regen pauses mid-action (Mobility Skills, Sprint/Traversal). Crouching costs no Energy, but stealth skills (e.g., invisibility from Stealth) sip it.
  • Combat: Fuels attacks and blocks—e.g., a Light Attack refunds on hit, a Charged Slash costs between a sip and gulp (Controls)—while shielding Health (Health Pip).
  • Builds:
    • Agility: Speeds regen, refunds more on varied combos—e.g., rogues sustain chains—from a moderate pool.
    • Strength: Shrinks pool but cuts costs—e.g., tanks conserve with efficient strikes—armor boosts resilience.
    • Gear: Light armor speeds regen; heavy reduces damage—e.g., armored mages balance larger pools with armor costs.

Challenges and Solutions

Energy presents hurdles:

  • Overload Risk: Broad use might drain too fast—e.g., tanks with small pools falter.
    • Solution: Combo refunds (varied hits more, capped) and tuned regen—e.g., agile builds refill faster—keep it viable.
  • Refund Bias: Varied combo refunds might still favor spam—e.g., rapid switches over depth.
    • Solution: Steeper decay on repeats—e.g., back-to-back moves drop off fast—and cap at modest hits (e.g., 5)—push creative chains.
  • Pool Size Extremes: Large mage pools might overpower tanks.
    • Solution: Moderate variation—e.g., tanks get efficiency, mages pay premiums—armor damage reduction balances hybrids.

Additional Context

Arenas influence Energy—cover aids regen, hazards spike costs—while class skills tweak it—stealth skills cost Energy, warriors lean on cheap normals. Movement like dashes or sprints burns it dynamically, tying Energy to pacing and tension per real-life effort.

Conclusion

The Energy Gauge in The Fighting Game flows as a natural force, fueling every action and shielding Health with a build-tuned pool that mirrors real-world exertion and resilience. Refunds on varied, well-timed combo hits—decaying with repetition—and armor’s damage reduction reward skill and strategy, ensuring every move pulses with purpose—balancing effort and endurance in a visceral, tactical dance.
