Resources & Gauges

Introduction

Every fight feels like a high-stakes resource battle where your choices have real weight. You feel the tension as your Energy drains with every aggressive dash and heavy swing, forcing you to decide whether to keep pushing or back off and recover. Land a perfect parry and your Burst gauge lights up — giving you that rush of turning the tables with a Wild Assault or Deflect Shield. Your Health pips hold strong as long as you manage your Energy buffer, while your Poise lets you shrug off stuns and unleash big supers. Damage Decay keeps long combos from feeling spammy, pushing you to mix up your attacks and stay creative. This is the feeling the Gauges system delivers: constant, meaningful decisions that make every exchange tense, rewarding, and deeply satisfying.

Goals

  • Foster tactical play by linking every action to meaningful resource costs.
  • Highlight build diversity through stats, gear, and skills (agile rogues vs. enduring tanks).
  • Ensure clarity with pips, bars, and blocks that convey status at a glance.
  • Scale seamlessly from 1v1 MVP to MOBA and MMORPG skirmishes.

Overview

Five tightly linked systems drive every moment of combat:

  • Health Pips — Your final line of survival, broken into discrete chunks so every hit feels meaningful.
  • Energy Gauge — The fuel for movement, attacks, and defense that protects your Health until it breaks.
  • Burst Gauge — The comeback resource that rewards survival and strong defensive reads with powerful reversals (see Burst Abilities for Wild Assault, Deflect Shield, and Psych Burst).
  • Poise Gauge — Your combat resolve that resists crowd control and powers high-impact specials.
  • Damage Decay — Prevents repetitive spam, forces creative offense, and reduces hitstun over long strings.

Core Guages

Health

Health measures a character’s raw endurance, visualized as pips—discrete vitality units akin to Killer Instinct’s segmented life bars.

  • Mechanics: Depletes in pips—normal attacks (e.g., Light Attack from Page 5) take one, specials or supers (Page 61) take more—while endurance boosts the count (up to 5-9 pips) and strength fortifies resilience. Damage over time (e.g., poison from Page 29) erodes pips gradually when Energy is low.
  • Combat Role: The last stand—e.g., a combo drains Energy, a follow-up hit chips a pip—leading to defeat at zero unless Burst intervenes.
  • Tactical Use: Urges Energy conservation—e.g., dodging a Heavy Slash (Page 5) to save pips—balanced by recovery like gray health.
Health Guage

The Health Gauge in The Fighting Game stands as the final measure of a character’s vitality, tracking their survival through a brutal high-fantasy, 3rd-person combat world. Represented as a row of discrete pips—each a fragile unit of life—it captures the tension of dwindling resilience, protected by the Energy Gauge until its shield falters. Featuring Current Health Pips, Shadow Health, Gray Health, and Empty Health Pips, this system balances recoverable and permanent damage, ensuring every hit resonates with consequence and every recovery demands strategy.

Goals

The Health Pip Gauge is crafted to:

  • Measure Vitality: Provide a segmented, tangible gauge of life, where each pip’s loss heightens stakes.
  • Sync with Energy: Rely on Energy protection until a critical threshold, then expose Health to direct damage.
  • Offer Recovery Paths: Enable players to reclaim lost health through universal mechanics, adding tactical depth.
  • Reflect Build Diversity: Adjust pip count and resilience via stats, gear, and skills, enhancing RPG customization.

Overview

The Health Pip Gauge depletes when the Energy Gauge falls below its breakpoint or when certain effects bypass Energy protection—each pip a full unit lost to attacks, with damage over time (DoTs) like bleeding or burning eroding fractions. It includes:

  • Current Health Pips: Active vitality units.
  • Shadow Health: Recent damage that slowly decays unless recovered by actions.
  • Gray Health: Lost portions that can regenerate over time or via skills.
  • Empty Health Pips: Permanently lost vitality, unrecoverable in-fight.

The final pip resists depletion—requiring significant force like a Special or Super—to trigger a downed state, a fleeting chance to rise or fall. Armor mitigates damage to both Energy and Health, while hurtbox-specific attacks (mid, low, high) or armor weak points modify impact, tying health to combat precision.

Core Mechanics

Health Pip

Health pips represent blocks of health that can be depleted in whole by taking unguarded hits:

  • Mechanics:
    • Health Pool: The Total health value is calculated as a pool, a value that can be diminsed in part via chip damage, damage over time, and other damage that may bypass the energy health breakpoint.
    • Partial Bypass: Effects like bleeding, poison or burning partially bypass Energy—e.g., a bleed ticks a pip fraction even with Energy this can be modifed by armor mitigation.
    • Health Pip’s: The health Pool is broken into pips that can be depleted in whole pips if the energy breakpoint has been surpassed.
    • Pips and Armour: Armor can increase pip count and health pool amounts providing additional resilience to different types of damage.
  • Combat Role: Health pips determine how many hits (and at what strength) you can survive once your breakpoint has been reached on your energy gauge.
  • Tactical Use: Creates an urgency when your energy breakpoint has been reached as health can be exposed and rescued quickly once this happens.

Health Pip Depletion

Health pips erode when Energy protection fails or specific damage slips through:

  • Mechanics:
    • Energy Breakpoint: Below the breakpoint (Energy Gauge), attacks can remove full pips—e.g., a Heavy Attack takes a full pip, a Super or super could take more—Stats can boost pip count and or the total health pool value.
    • Armor Modifiers: Could protect health pip loss over the regions they occupy (Mid, Low, High)
  • Combat Role: Avoiding your health from depleting to zero and reduscing the opponent's health to zero is the objective of a match and leads directly to a win or defeat condition.
  • Tactical Use: Urges Energy maintenance—e.g., dodging to preserve pips—or risks bypass damage—e.g., bleeding ignores some Energy.

Shadow, Gray, and Empty Health

These universal states track damage aftermath:

  • Mechanics:
    • Shadow Health: Fresh attack damage leaves partial shadow health, which slowly decays to match current pips unless recovered by actions—e.g., hitting foes refills a portion—This is a form of recoverable “missing” health that can be regenerated for a brief window after it is lost.
    • Gray Health: Lost pips from damage can also leave gray health, recoverable over time passively or via skills—e.g., a special attack could regenerate a portion of gray health— this is a form of recoverable “missing” health that can remain available to regenerate unless the player is defeated.
    • Empty Health Pips: Fully depleted pips turn empty, unrecoverable in-fight.
  • Combat Role: Allows recovery—e.g., an effective counterattack refills some recently lost health by recovering shadow health—or a specific skill restores missing —e.g., gray regenerates slowly over time—while empty pips signal attrition.
  • Tactical Use: Promotes aggression to recover shadow health before it fades away, or the use of specific skills to recover Gray health or disengaging to attempt to recover. This could result in being too predictable or create opportunities to bait opponents.

Passive Health Regen

Health lost as Gray health regenerates over time passively to mitigate some damage types.

  • Mechanics:
    • Gray Health: Left behind from Damage, especially common with Damage over time, or damage that bypasses energy before it has reached it breakpoint.
    • Passive Regeneration: Greay Health will regenerate to Health over time based on gear, skills and buffs. This can outpace Damage over time in some cases.
  • Combat Role: Creates more reason to retreat and attempt to recover, helps mitigate aspects of Damage over time.
  • Tactical Use: Recover some health on retreat..

The Last Pip

The Final Pip of health is more resilient than the rest. While it can be overwhelmed by Damage over time that is heavier than the regeneration, breaking through the final pip requires a significantly slow attack, such as an impact, heavy special or super to break through the Pip and down the opponent.

  • Mechanics: The last pip resists lighter damage forcing slower stronger attacks to act as the killing blow. Attacks that break multiple pips can break through the final pip if the remaining pips are less or equal to what it would normally deplete.
  • Combat Role: Offers a final possibility to turn the tides.
  • Tactical Use: .

Downed State

Depleting the final pip triggers a downed state, a fragile lifeline:

  • Mechanics: The last pip resists loss—e.g., only stronger attacks break it—entering a state where Poise and Energy regen to rise or face a finishing blow.
  • Combat Role: Offers an opportunity to be saved in group combat.
  • Tactical Use: likely none for now, at least in 1v1 it is unlikely that a player would not finish of a downed opponent, this feature is more to lay the ground work for group combat and reviving downed allies.

Integration with Game Systems

The Health Pip Gauge ties into core mechanics:

  • Energy: Protects health until the breakpoint—e.g., a block drains Energy, sparing pips (Energy Gauge)—below which pips take full hits; bypass effects like bleeding erode both.
  • Combat: Normals take one pip—e.g., a Light Attack—Specials or Supers more—e.g., a Super cuts two (Combat sections)—DoTs bypass partially—e.g., burn ticks despite Energy.
  • Builds:
    • Fortitude: Increases pip count—e.g., tanks boast 7-9 vs. 5-6 base.
    • Agility: Boosts evasion, preserving pips—e.g., rogues dodge mid-hits.
    • Gear: Armor reduces pip loss—e.g., heavy plate cuts a Heavy Attack’s impact—light gear leans on mobility.
  • Movement: Actions like sprinting (Sprint/Traversal) drain Energy, risking pip exposure—e.g., over-sprinting depletes the shield (Energy Gauge).

Challenges and Solutions

Health pips present hurdles:

  • Depletion Clarity: Full-pip loss below breakpoint might confuse—e.g., when Energy fails mid-combo.
    • Solution: Flash pips below breakpoint—e.g., a red pulse—via UI cues for clarity.
  • Downed State Balance: Regen vs. vulnerability could skew—e.g., too safe or harsh.
    • Solution: Set a timed window—e.g., 5 seconds—to rise, simplifying stakes without extra resources.
  • Bypass Ambiguity: Partial bypass (e.g., bleeding) might muddle—e.g., when does Energy fail?
    • Solution: Define bypass triggers—e.g., DoTs only—and scale with Energy level—e.g., higher Energy cuts bleed damage.

Additional Context

Arenas shape health—hazards (e.g., lava) tax pips directly, cover aids gray regen—while class skills tweak recovery—warriors endure more pips, rogues regen gray faster—but shadow and gray remain universal damage states. Energy’s protective role (Energy Gauge) and movement costs (Mobility Skills) tie health to pacing, amplifying combat tension.

Conclusion

The Health Pip Gauge in The Fighting Game beats as the fragile core of survival, shielded by Energy until the breakpoint cracks. With current pips enduring hits, shadow and gray offering universal recovery from damage taken, and empty pips marking scars, it ensures every strike cuts deep—pushing players to wield Energy and armor to fend off the inevitable, in a relentless dance of resilience and resolve.

Energy

Energy powers every move, a bar that fuels attacks, movement, and defense while shielding Health, akin to Dark Souls’s stamina but with broader utility.

  • Mechanics: Drains with use—light moves (e.g., jab from Page 5) sip, sprints (Page 12) or specials gulp—regenerating quickly when idle, pausing briefly after hits. Successful hits (not blocked) refund Energy—weighted toward normals (e.g., Light Attack refunds more than a Special from Page 57), with early hits refunding more, late hits less. Pool size varies—tanks have smaller pools with cheaper attacks, mages larger pools with costlier ones—agility speeds regen, weight slows it. A midpoint breakpoint signals Health vulnerability.
  • Combat Role: Fuels mobility (e.g., Dash from Page 11), attacks (e.g., Charged Slash from Page 5), and blocks—e.g., a Climb (Page 12) costs less for agile builds—while buffering Health.
  • Tactical Use: Rewards precision—e.g., early jabs refund Energy—or pacing, risking depletion for big plays—e.g., a mage’s costly spell taps a larger pool.
Energy Guage

The Energy Gauge in The Fighting Game fuels the pulse of action and resilience, a versatile resource that powers every strike, dash, and sprint while shielding Health in its high-fantasy, 3rd-person combat world. Designed as a natural mechanic—reflecting real-life stamina and the effort to mitigate damage—it drives the game’s tactical rhythm, rewarding precision and punishing exhaustion. Displayed as a bar with a midpoint breakpoint, Energy balances exertion and survival, shaped by build-specific traits and combat flow.

Goals

The Energy Gauge system is crafted to:

  • Unify Resource Use: Consolidate all actions—combat, movement, defense—into a single, intuitive pool, simplifying management with a natural feel.
  • Shield Health: Absorb damage to delay Health loss, mirroring energy’s role in deflecting or enduring blows.
  • Reward Skill: Refund Energy on successful combo hits, encouraging varied, well-timed attacks over repetition.
  • Reflect Customization: Adjust pool size, costs, and regeneration to suit playstyles—e.g., efficient tanks vs. expansive mages—with balanced tradeoffs.

Overview

The Energy Gauge powers every action—light jabs, dashes, sprints, and specials—while acting as a shield that depletes before Health takes lasting harm, akin to using stamina to position armor or dodge blows until exhaustion sets in. Energy drains with use—light moves sip, heavy attacks draw modestly, sprints steadily drain, and specials gulp—regenerating when idle, with a brief pause after hits. Successful hits (not blocked) refund Energy, favoring varied combos over repeats, with early hits refunding more—a cap limits excess, excluding specials and supers from bonuses. Pool sizes vary: tanks wield smaller pools with cheaper moves, mages larger ones with costlier casts—armor reduces damage to both, balancing depletion.

Core Mechanics

Energy as Fuel

Energy powers exertion, embodying the effort of combat and movement:

  • Mechanics: Costs scale naturally—light attacks (e.g., Light Attack from Controls) sip Energy, heavy attacks (e.g., Heavy Attack) sit between a sip and gulp, sprints (Sprint/Traversal) steadily drain (less out of combat), and specials or supers (Combat sections) gulp heavily. Costs adjust by build—tanks pay less for normals, mages more for spells—while agility speeds regen and heavy gear slows it. Crouching (Grounded Movement) costs no Energy but can trigger stealth skills with costs (Stealth).
  • Combat Role: Fuels every move—e.g., a Dash sips Energy, a Sprint steadily drains—driving combat and movement pacing.
  • Tactical Use: Encourages mixing light, efficient moves with heavier spends—e.g., a tank chains cheap jabs, a mage risks a gulp-worthy spell—offset by depletion risks.

Energy as Health Shield

Energy absorbs damage before Health, reflecting stamina’s role in reducing bodily harm:

  • Mechanics: Takes hits first—e.g., a Heavy Attack drains Energy—stopping at the midpoint breakpoint unless depleted, exposing Health. Armor reduces Energy and Health damage—e.g., a plated warrior loses less per hit—grey Energy tracks temporary loss, erased by hits.
  • Combat Role: Shields Health—e.g., a combo saps Energy, not pips—until the breakpoint, after which Health takes the brunt (Health Pip).
  • Tactical Use: Urges maintaining Energy—e.g., dodging to keep the shield—or risks permanent damage when exhausted—e.g., a stab hits Health directly.

Regeneration and Refunds

Energy replenishes and rewards naturally:

  • Regeneration: Restores quickly when idle—faster for agile builds—pausing briefly after damage (e.g., a short delay post-hit), resuming below the breakpoint to ensure action viability.
  • Refunds: Successful hits (not blocked) refund Energy—normals refund more than specials/supers (e.g., a jab refunds a decent chunk, a Super none), with early hits refunding more, late hits less. Combo variety boosts refunds—repeating attacks decays the refund (e.g., back-to-back jabs drop off fast), varied hits increase it (e.g., jab-to-slash gains more)—capped after a set hit count, excluding specials/supers from bonuses.
  • Tactical Use: Rewards precision—e.g., varied combos sustain a rogue—or pacing—e.g., pausing to regen—balanced by combo decay and cap.

Build-Specific Pool Sizes

Energy pool size reflects effort capacity:

  • Mechanics: Tanks have smaller pools (e.g., suited for fewer, cheaper moves), mages larger pools (e.g., for costlier spells)—agility boosts regen, strength cuts costs slightly. Armor reduces damage—hybrid builds (e.g., armored mages) balance pool size with armor tradeoffs—e.g., heavier gear might shrink the pool but lessen damage taken.
  • Combat Role: Tanks conserve with low-cost normals—e.g., a Heavy Attack costs less—mages burn through for big spells—e.g., a Special costs more.
  • Tactical Use: Lets tanks endure with efficiency—e.g., chaining refunded hits—while mages wield power—e.g., a spell barrage—balanced by regen and armor effects.

Integration with Game Systems

The Energy Gauge syncs with core mechanics:

  • Movement: Powers dashes, sprints, or climbs—e.g., a Dash sips Energy, a Sprint steadily drains (less out of combat)—regen pauses mid-action (Mobility Skills, Sprint/Traversal). Crouching costs no Energy, but stealth skills (e.g., invisibility from Stealth) sip it.
  • Combat: Fuels attacks and blocks—e.g., a Light Attack refunds on hit, a Charged Slash costs between a sip and gulp (Controls)—while shielding Health (Health Pip).
  • Builds:
    • Agility: Speeds regen, refunds more on varied combos—e.g., rogues sustain chains—from a moderate pool.
    • Strength: Shrinks pool but cuts costs—e.g., tanks conserve with efficient strikes—armor boosts resilience.
    • Gear: Light armor speeds regen; heavy reduces damage—e.g., armored mages balance larger pools with armor costs.

Challenges and Solutions

Energy presents hurdles:

  • Overload Risk: Broad use might drain too fast—e.g., tanks with small pools falter.
    • Solution: Combo refunds (varied hits more, capped) and tuned regen—e.g., agile builds refill faster—keep it viable.
  • Refund Bias: Varied combo refunds might still favor spam—e.g., rapid switches over depth.
    • Solution: Steeper decay on repeats—e.g., back-to-back moves drop off fast—and cap at modest hits (e.g., 5)—push creative chains.
  • Pool Size Extremes: Large mage pools might overpower tanks.
    • Solution: Moderate variation—e.g., tanks get efficiency, mages pay premiums—armor damage reduction balances hybrids.

Additional Context

Arenas influence Energy—cover aids regen, hazards spike costs—while class skills tweak it—stealth skills cost Energy, warriors lean on cheap normals. Movement like dashes or sprints burns it dynamically, tying Energy to pacing and tension per real-life effort.

Conclusion

The Energy Gauge in The Fighting Game flows as a natural force, fueling every action and shielding Health with a build-tuned pool that mirrors real-world exertion and resilience. Refunds on varied, well-timed combo hits—decaying with repetition—and armor’s damage reduction reward skill and strategy, ensuring every move pulses with purpose—balancing effort and endurance in a visceral, tactical dance.

  • Combat Role: Triggers escapes or disruptions—e.g., a Psych Burst (Page 20) breaks a combo—costing the bar or a portion (e.g., 50% for Deflect Shield, Page 19).

Burst Guage

The Burst Gauge in The Fighting Game glows as a spark of defiance, a resource that fuels clutch escapes and disruptions in its high-fantasy, 3rd-person combat arena. Starting full and pulsing with potential, it builds through damage taken and time endured, offering players a way to break enemy pressure or defy the odds—targeting 1-2 uses per fight. Inspired by Guilty Gear’s Burst system, it rewards resilience and timing, weaving into Energy and Health dynamics to amplify the game’s tactical depth with build-specific flair.

Goals

The Burst Gauge system strives to:

  • Enable Clutch Plays: Offer tools to escape or disrupt dire moments, enriching combat’s dramatic swings.
  • Reward Adversity: Fill through damage and persistence, ensuring availability aligns with fight intensity—1-2 impactful uses.
  • Support Build Diversity: Tailor gain rates and usability to stats, gear, and skills, reflecting player styles.
  • Balance Power: Provide potent options without dominating, tempered by cost and paced build-up.

Overview

The Burst Gauge begins full at match start—with the remaining carried over between rounds—and fills passively over time (faster at low Health percentages) or from damage taken to Energy or Health (less for energy), visualized as a glowing bar that pulses with urgency. It powers three moves: Wild Assault (50% cost, aggressive disruption), Deflect Shield (50% cost, projectile counter), and Psych Burst (100% cost, combo breaker or reset), each draining the bar to shift momentum. Its immediate availability deepens early tactics while preserving late-game desperation.

Core Mechanics

Burst Generation

The Burst Gauge builds through resilience and punishment:

  • Mechanics:
    • Passive Fill: Replenishes over time—slower at high Health (100%-51%), average at mid-range (50%-26%), faster at low (~25% or less)—e.g., a low-Health tank fills quicker than a full-Health rogue.
    • Damage Taken: Increases with damage to Energy or Health—e.g., a Light Attack adds a sliver, a Super more while damage taken to energy replenishes less than damage taken to health.
    • Build Influence: Builds could influence bursts gain rate—e.g., a mage build might fill faster from Energy instead of health damage taken.
  • Combat Role: Fuels a comeback—e.g., enduring a combo—or rewards pacing—e.g., stalling at low Health—starting full for immediate depth.
  • Tactical Use: Offers early options—e.g., Wild Assault on opener—or late resets—e.g., Psych Burst when stuck in a devastating combo—balanced by limited use.

Burst Usage

The Burst Gauge powers three tactical moves, echoing Guilty Gear’s clutch utility:

  • Wild Assault (50%): An aggressive strike—e.g., a charging blow—disrupts foes, dealing damage and reducing their burst gauge by a small amount keeping it from being used to psych burst.
    • Role: Shifts momentum—e.g., breaking pressure—risking a parry or dodge wasting it.
  • Deflect Shield (50%): A defensive burst—e.g., countering a projectile—blocks ranged and melee threats.
    • Role: Protects—e.g., negating a spell—offset by situational use.
  • Psych Burst (100%): A full-bar reset—e.g., escaping a combo—disrupts or repositions, offering a second wind (Psych Burst).
    • Role: Saves from combos—e.g., breaking a kill chain—limited by full-bar cost and later availability.
  • Tactical Use: Fuels early aggression—e.g., Wild Assault at 50%—or late desperation—e.g., Psych Burst post-recharge—balanced by cost and foe response.

Integration with Game Systems

The Burst Gauge syncs with mechanics:

  • Energy: Fills from Energy damage—e.g., a blocked Dash adds Burst (Mobility Skills)—and powers moves—e.g., Wild Assault costs Energy too (Controls).
  • Health: Accelerates fill at low percentages—e.g., below 25% speeds gain (Health Pip)—tying to survival stakes.
  • Builds:
    • Agility: Speeds gain—e.g., rogues fill from dodged hits—enhancing mobility (Mobility Skills).
    • Fortitude: Ties to Health pool—e.g., tanks gain as percentages drop—boosting endurance (Health Pip).
    • Gear: Light armor aids gain; heavy bolsters resilience—balancing style (Controls).
  • Combat: Fuels escapes/disruptions—e.g., Psych Burst breaks a combo (Psych Burst)—or counters—e.g., Deflect Shield parries a Special (Combat sections)—enhancing clutch moments.

Challenges and Solutions

Burst presents hurdles:

  • Build Disparity: some builds might fill too fast—e.g., outpacing others early.
    • Solution: Normalize via Health percentage—e.g., tanks gain as pips drop—ensuring 1-2 uses across styles with prototyping to tune.
  • Clarity: Fill vs. use might confuse—e.g., when’s Psych Burst ready?
    • Solution: Glow scales with fill—e.g., full bar pulses brightly—enhancing UI readability.

Additional Context

Arenas shape Burst—hazards speed fill via damage (e.g., lava hits), cover slows it by reducing hits—while skills tweak it—rogues gain from evasion, warriors from enduring blows—per class diversity concepts. Energy costs (Energy Gauge) and Health thresholds (Health Pip) tie Burst to pacing, amplifying its clutch role.

The Burst Gauge in The Fighting Game shines as a spark of defiance, filling through damage and time to power escapes and disruptions with a bar ready from the start. Poise Guage

Introduction

The Poise Gauge in The Fighting Game represents a fighter’s Combat Resolve, displayed as a bar broken into five segmented blocks much like tension in Guilty Gear Xrd. It acts as a resource for powerful abilities—like Roman Cancels, Supers, and Faultless Defense—while also providing passive benefits such as reduced damage and shorter control effect durations. Starting at its midpoint (2.5 blocks), the gauge regenerates toward this point over time and shifts based on combat actions, promoting dynamic and strategic play.

Goals

The Poise Gauge aims to:

  • Enable Tactical Flexibility: Fuel Supers, Roman Cancels, and Faultless Defense with a limited, renewable resource.
  • Provide Passive Resilience: Reduce incoming damage and control effect durations based on gauge levels.
  • Encourage Active Play: Grow through offensive and defensive actions, while penalizing passivity or poor management.
  • Offer Customization: Allow gear and skills to tweak regeneration, capacity, and ability costs.

Overview

The Poise Gauge begins at 2.5 blocks (midpoint of Block 3), its natural equilibrium. It increases with actions like landing hits or blocking and decreases when using abilities or passively when above the midpoint. Passive benefits scale with its level:

  • Damage Reduction: Grows as Poise drops, enhancing durability.
  • Control Duration Reduction: Shortens stun or knockdown times, strongest at full gauge.

The Poise Guage acts as a spendable resource for:

  • Supers (3-5)
  • Faultless Guard (steady drain)
  • Guarded attacks (2-4)
  • Roman Cancels (1-3)

A Poise Break occurs when the gauge empties, halting regeneration and stripping passive benefits, making fighters more susceptible to damage and control effects.

Core Mechanics

Poise Fluctuation

  • Building Poise:
    • Landing attacks (e.g., Light Attack adds a small amount).
    • Blocking hits (e.g., blocking a Heavy Attack adds a moderate amount).
    • Parrying (e.g., a perfect parry adds a full block).
    • being hit while below 2.5 blocks (unless at 0 poise)
  • Depleting Poise:
    • Using Supers (3-5 blocks).
    • Activating Roman Cancels (1-3 blocks).
    • Engaging Faultless Defense (continuous drain).
    • Guarded attacks (2-4 blocks)
    • Being countered or parried.
    • Being hit while above 2.5 blocks
  • Passive Regeneration: Moves toward 2.5 blocks—rising if below, falling if above—unless at zero (delayed by Poise Break).

Supers

  • Costs 3-5 blocks
  • Effect: Use of the strongest attacks available to the player.

Faultless Defense

  • Cost: Initial drain plus ongoing drain while active.
  • Effect: Improves guarding but risks Poise Break if overextended.

Guarded Attacks

  • Costs 2-4 blocks
  • Effect: Interrupt an opponent from blockstun

Roman Cancels

  • Cost: 1-3 blocks (e.g., Red Roman Cancel: 1 block, Blue: 2 blocks).
  • Effect: Cancels animations for tactical resets or combo extensions, with variants (Blue, Red, Purple, Yellow) offering unique effects like slowdown or defense boosts.

Passive Benefits

  • Damage Reduction: Increases as Poise decreases—low Poise offers more protection.
  • Control Duration Reduction: Scales with Poise—full gauge greatly reduces stun or knockdown times. At max, a hit might skip hitstun, draining Poise instead.

Poise Break

  • Trigger: Gauge reaches zero, pausing regeneration and removing passive benefits.
  • Risk: Full/extended control effect durations and no damage reduction until recovery begins.

Control attacks (possibly)

  • Control attacks could also cost poise, perhaps not in blocks but as a drained value like the energy bar, limiting access to other options the same way faultless defense does.
  • Costs could scale with specials being significantly more expensive on poise.
  • This could serve as an alternative or in addition to energy drain and open up control-focused builds

Customization and Combat Dynamics

  • Gear and Skills:
    • Increase regeneration speed.
    • Reduce ability costs or expand the functionality of ability at additional cost..
  • Playstyle Impact:
    • Aggressive players build Poise quickly for cancels and Supers.
    • Defensive players leverage Faultless Defense and regeneration control.

Integration with Game Systems

  • Energy: Drives actions that build Poise.
  • Health: Low health slows regeneration, heightening tension.
  • Combat Flow: Balances offense (cancels, Supers) with defense (Faultless Defense, passive benefits).

Challenges and Solutions

  • Challenge: The midpoint regeneration (draining above 2.5 blocks) might confuse players.
    • Solution: Add tutorial hints or UI indicators (e.g., a glowing midpoint) to highlight its neutral state.
  • Challenge: Poise Break could feel too punishing, leaving players overly vulnerable at zero Poise.
    • Solution: Shorten the break delay or tie it to health—e.g., higher health reduces the penalty duration.
  • Challenge: Faultless Defense’s steady drain might deplete Poise too fast.
    • Solution: Offer gear to lower the drain rate or grant Poise boosts for successful blocks.

Additional Context

  • Environment: Areas with pressure zones boost Poise gain through increased combat.
  • Build Flexibility: Gear and skills tailor the gauge to offensive or defensive preferences.

Conclusion

The Poise Gauge links resilience to action, powering key abilities while offering passive protection. Its midpoint regeneration and Poise Break mechanics reward active engagement and tactical depth, making it a cornerstone of The Fighting Game’s combat system.

Damage Decay

Damage Decay is the system that stops you from spamming the same attack over and over and feeling unstoppable. It gradually reduces damage (and hitstun) the longer you stick to the same move or string, pushing you to mix things up and keep your offense fresh and unpredictable. In big fights it also quickly lowers the threat from multiple opponents, so you’re never just getting chipped to death by the same Light Attack from three enemies.

Goals

  • Encourage creative, varied offense instead of repetitive spam.
  • Reward shorter, escalating combos that feel skillful and satisfying.
  • Make multi-opponent fights more manageable and less overwhelming.
  • Tie directly into Energy refunds and Health protection so the whole gauge system feels connected.

Overview
Damage Decay applies to repeated identical attacks (especially same type: Light or Heavy) and to a lesser degree same-state attacks. It reduces both damage to Energy and hitstun duration the longer the string goes. The effect is strongest on long combos or when multiple enemies use the same move, forcing you to adapt on the fly. This keeps fights dynamic and prevents any single attack from becoming a “win button.”

Core Mechanics

  • Damage Reduction: Repeated Light/Heavy attacks lose potency the more you use them in a string (weighted heaviest toward identical moves).
  • Hitstun Reduction: The longer the string, the less hitstun each hit applies — making it easier for the defender to escape or counter.
  • Multi-Opponent Scaling: Identical attacks from multiple enemies decay extremely fast, preventing “death by a thousand cuts” from group spam.
  • Interaction with Meaty: Meaty attacks still grant their normal blockstun advantage, but the decay still applies to follow-ups, rewarding smart timing over mindless repetition.

Integration with Game Systems

  • Energy: Decay lowers damage to Energy, which means more Energy refunds on varied combos — tying directly into Energy Gauge pacing.
  • Health Pips: Less chip damage over time protects your Health buffer longer.
  • Poise & Burst: Shorter hitstun from decay gives more windows to build Poise or trigger Burst reversals.
  • Combat Triangle & States: Encourages switching between Attack types and states (Base → Impact → Guard) to reset decay.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Feels Punishing on Long Combos → Solution: Decay ramps gradually and resets on state changes or different attack types — rewarding creativity, not punishing skill.
  • Hard to Read in Fast Fights → Solution: Subtle VFX (fading damage numbers + shorter hitsparks) and training mode decay meter make it crystal clear.
  • Multi-Opponent Over-Tuning → Solution: Tunable per build (tanks resist group decay better) — easy to adjust in Blueprints.

Additional Context
Damage Decay works beautifully with Meaty attacks and Roman Cancels — letting you reset decay mid-combo for explosive payoff. In the full MMORPG it will also scale to large-scale PvP so zergs can’t just faceroll the same move forever.

Conclusion
Damage Decay keeps combat feeling alive and fair. It stops any single attack from dominating, pushes you to master your full toolkit, and makes every fight feel like a real back-and-forth dance — exactly what we want for the 1v1 MVP and beyond.

Integration with Game Systems

Gauges interlink with mechanics:

  • Movement: Energy drives dashes (Page 11) or climbs (Page 12)—e.g., agile builds cost less—Poise steadies knockback mid-sprint.
  • Combat: Energy fuels strikes (e.g., Light Attack from Page 5, refunded on hit), Burst escapes (Page 19), Poise powers specials—e.g., a refunded jab into a Burst dodge.
  • Builds:
    • Agility: Speeds Energy regen, boosts Burst—e.g., rogues recover mid-fight—smaller pools offset by cost efficiency.
    • Strength: Adds Poise blocks, Health pips—e.g., tanks endure longer—smaller Energy pools with cheaper moves.
    • Gear: Light armor speeds Energy regen; heavy boosts Poise—blending styles with tradeoffs (e.g., armored mages balance pool size and armor cost).
  • Stealth: Energy sustains quiet moves (Page 13)—e.g., crouching sips—enhancing ambushes.

Challenges and Solutions

Gauges present hurdles:

  • Energy Overload: Broad use might drain too fast—e.g., tanks with small pools falter.
    • Solution: Refunds on successful hits (normals weighted higher, early hits more) and tuned regen—e.g., agile builds refill faster—keep it fluid.
  • Burst Accessibility: Slow build could delay clutch plays.
    • Solution: Damage-driven gain (Energy/Health) plus parry boosts ensure 2-3 uses—consistent and fight-paced.
  • Poise Clarity: Five-block limit might confuse in chaos—e.g., super timing mid-fight.
    • Solution: Glowing block UI and “Combat Resolve” name clarify—familiar from Tension/Drive—easing onboarding.

Additional Context

Arenas amplify gauges—cover aids Energy regen, hazards tax Health. Class skills tweak them—stealth cuts Energy use, warriors stack Poise—while movement like sprints or dashes shifts their flow, tying resources to the game’s rhythm.

Conclusion

Gauges in The Fighting Game pulse with strategic life, blending Health’s endurance, Energy’s versatility, Burst’s clutch power, and Poise’s resolve into a unified system. Rooted in combat, movement, and builds—amplified by Energy refunds on well-timed hits—they turn every action into a calculated play, ensuring resources fuel both survival and supremacy with every tense exchange.
