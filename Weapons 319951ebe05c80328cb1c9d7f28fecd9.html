Weapons

Weapons

Goals

The weapon system is designed to:

  • Enhance Tactical Variety: Provide diverse tools that shape combat through range, radius, and environmental impact, supporting unique playstyles.
  • Streamline Combat Flow: Integrate seamless auto-switching tied to states and movesets, balancing fighting game responsiveness with RPG customization.
  • Enable Scalability: Ensure weapons function in 1v1 duels and scale naturally to group combat, laying groundwork for summons and future MMORPG expansions.
  • Reflect Customization: Tie weapons to gear slots and skill trees, offering depth without progression grind, accessible to all players.

Overview

Weapons in The Fighting Game are the player’s tools of combat, merging the precision of fighting games with the versatility of action RPGs in a 3rd-person over-the-shoulder perspective. Equipped via armor-defined slots (e.g., back, hips, legs), weapons auto-switch based on the active state (Base, Impact, Focus, Guard) and moveset, delivering attacks with inherent AoE and friendly fire risk. Each weapon carries distinct physical properties—slash, pierce, blunt, or thrown—shaping its range, damage, and environmental impact (e.g., blunt shatters cover, pierce pins objects). The initial arsenal consists of basic historical weapons (e.g., longsword, warhammer, spear), offering variety without magical embellishments, with potential for expansion over time. Energy costs scale with weight, ensuring resource tension, while subtle Poise ties enhance tactical depth. Losing a weapon via disarming—a temporary control effect—sends it flying 5-10 meters, forcing players to a weaker universal hand-to-hand set unless custom unarmed moves are slotted, with fist builds offering immunity at a cost. This system supports the core loop’s rock-paper-scissors dynamic while preparing for multi-foe engagements.

Core Weapon System

Weapons are equipped through gear slots determined by armor, typically ranging from 2-4 slots (e.g., back for a bow, left hip for a sword, right hip for a quiver, leg for a dagger). Each slot holds one weapon, auto-switching when its tied state or skill activates—e.g., a warhammer swaps in for an Impact State ground slam, a dagger for a Focus State grab. The skill tree refines this, locking specific weapons to moves (e.g., a leg-slot dagger for a grab special).

  • Damage Types: Weapons wield physical properties like Slash (cutting arcs), Pierce (narrow thrusts), Blunt (wide smashes), or Thrown (projectile range)—e.g., a longsword slashes, a javelin pierces and throws. Attacks are state-agnostic, with movesets defining execution (e.g., a mace swings directionally in Base or precisely in Aimed).
  • Pool and Variety: The starting arsenal features basic historical weapons (e.g., longsword, warhammer, spear, shortbow), each with unique stats (damage, weight, reach). No grind—newbies and veterans access the same pool, expandable via future updates (e.g., “new armory relics”).
  • Environmental Roles: Damage types interact with arenas—Blunt breaks cover (e.g., smashing crates), Pierce pins objects (e.g., sticking a spear in a wall), Thrown strikes from afar, enhancing tactical options.
  • Disarmed State: When disarmed (e.g., via an Impact State control attack), the active weapon flies 5-10 meters in a random direction, requiring proximity retrieval to re-equip. Players revert to a universal hand-to-hand moveset (e.g., weak jabs, kicks) unless custom unarmed skills are slotted in other states (e.g., Impact’s unique fist slams persist if pre-set). Fists, equippable as a Blunt subtype, offer immunity to disarming with unique moves when chosen, but carry drawbacks (e.g., shorter reach), ensuring weapon loss remains detrimental.

Integration with Combat

Weapons tie into the combat ecosystem fluidly:

  • States: Auto-switching aligns with Base (directional adaptability), Impact (disruption), Focus (precision/AoE), and Guard (defense). A shortbow’s range thrives in Focus, a mace’s smash in Impact—movesets enforce compatibility (e.g., no bows for ground slams). Disarmed players retain custom unarmed moves in unaffected states.
  • Gauges: Energy scales with weight—light daggers cost less, heavy warhammers drain more—while blunt weapons subtly boost Poise on hit. Swap delays (e.g., 0.2-0.5 seconds for distant slots) add risk, extended slightly when retrieving a disarmed weapon.
  • Core Loop: AoE attacks with friendly fire reinforce Prediction (e.g., baiting foes into a mace sweep) and Advantage (e.g., chaining multi-hits). Disarming disrupts Advantage (e.g., weaker combos) and shifts to Disadvantage, pushing retrieval or adaptation. Environmental interplay (e.g., breaking cover in Neutral) deepens strategy.
  • Scalability: Wide-ranging weapons (e.g., warhammers, shortbows) excel against summons or groups, while narrow ones (e.g., daggers, spears) focus single targets. Disarming in group scenarios cripples multi-target threats (e.g., a summoner’s bow), enhancing tactical play.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Swap Delay Risk: Slow switches (e.g., from back slot) or retrieval could disrupt combat flow.

    Solution: Cap delays at 0.5 seconds, reducible via skill tree cancels (e.g., 0 delay for 5 points). Retrieval proximity auto-equips (e.g., 1-meter radius), keeping pacing brisk.

  • Slot Overload: Too many weapons might confuse players or skew balance.

    Solution: Limit slots to 4 max, with armor variety (e.g., light = 2, heavy = 4) and weight penalties (e.g., +10% Energy cost per slot) curbing excess.

  • Moveset Mismatch: Skills requiring unfit weapons (e.g., shortbow for a ground slam) could frustrate customization.

    Solution: Skill tree flags compatible types (e.g., “ground-slam” skills need Blunt/Slash), with UI cues guiding slotting; disarmed state preserves custom unarmed options where slotted.

  • Disarmed Balance: Universal hand-to-hand might feel too weak, fists too niche.

    Solution: Tune unarmed moves to low damage/range (e.g., 50% of weapon norms), fists stronger but limited (e.g., 75% damage, half reach). High Energy cost for disarming (e.g., 20%) balances its power.

Conclusions

The weapon system delivers a streamlined yet versatile foundation, blending fighting game fluidity with RPG customization through slot-based auto-switching and state-driven movesets. Basic historical weapons (e.g., warhammer for Impact, smashing cover and foes) ensure accessibility for prototyping, while AoE, friendly fire, and environmental roles lay groundwork for group combat and MMORPG scalability. The Disarmed state—triggered by control attacks, sending weapons 5-10 meters—adds consequence, reverting players to weak universal fists unless custom unarmed moves or equipped fists (immune but short-ranged) are slotted, keeping weapons vital. Energy and Poise ties deepen strategy without complexity, supporting the shoto archetype for initial testing with room to grow.
