Knockdown to Prone

Knockdown to Prone

Goals

The Knockdown to Prone system is designed to:

  • Differentiate Knockdown Types: Distinguish soft and hard knockdowns for varied combat outcomes, enhancing tactical depth.
  • Enable Prone Dynamics: Offer a vulnerable yet viable prone state with unique combat and recovery options, enriching player choice.
  • Integrate with Core Mechanics: Tie knockdowns and prone recovery to "Core Loop"’s Disadvantage phase, rewarding opponent pressure (e.g., okizeme) and defensive resilience.
  • Reflect High-Fantasy Theme: Frame prone as a gladiatorial struggle—down but not out—mirroring The Fighting Game’s cinematic chaos.

Overview

Knockdown to Prone governs how characters fall and recover in The Fighting Game’s 3rd-person, high-fantasy battles. Most attacks trigger a soft knockdown, where the character instantly rolls backward (5f) and auto-recovers to standing—akin to a fighting game throw—unless the player holds crouch to stay prone. Specific moves (e.g., Impact State overheads) cause a hard knockdown, dropping the character to the ground with hitstun (15-20f, move-dependent), landing on their back or stomach based on attack direction (front = stomach, back = back). In prone, players wield a unique moveset: back position enables melee attacks and blocks (high/mid), stomach allows ranged attacks, with blocking flipping to back via a startup animation (5f). Prone Recovery offers multiple exits—crouch to roll into crouch, jump (tap to stand, hold to leap), or dodge to roll upright—each with hitbox-protected startup (10f invuln on key hitboxes), though hits while prone reset to soft knockdown, encouraging okizeme over combo extension. Voluntary prone entry (D-pad Down/X) has a startup (~5-10f), balancing tactical drops. This system integrates with "Impact State" (e.g., hard knockdown setups) and "Grappling State Skills" (e.g., prone grab vulnerability), amplifying "Core Loop"’s Advantage-to-Disadvantage tension with a grounded, strategic twist.

Key Features

  • Soft Knockdown: Instant backward roll (~5f invuln), auto-stands unless crouch held by frame 3 to stay prone—mimics throw recovery (e.g., Guilty Gear’s quick pop-up).
  • Hard Knockdown: Hitstun (~15-20f, varies by move) drops player prone—stomach (front attack) or back (back attack)—with HUD tag ("HARD") for clarity.
  • Prone States:
    • Back: Melee attacks/blocks (high/mid focus, e.g., kicks upward), reduced speed (~+5f startup), vulnerable at feet.
    • Stomach: Ranged attacks (e.g., projectiles), blocking flips to back (~5f startup, head invuln during flip), weak to rear/above.
  • Prone Recovery:
    • Crouch: Back rolls forward to crouch, stomach pushes up/back to crouch (~10f, hitbox-protected startup).
    • Jump: Tap stands (10f), hold leaps (15f, hitbox-protected startup).
    • Dodge: Rolls to stand (~15f, hitbox-protected startup).
    • Hits in first ~5f of idle prone reset to soft knockdown.
  • Voluntary Prone: D-pad Down/X drops to stomach (~5-10f startup), same recovery options.
  • Okizeme Subsystem: Prone state primes wakeup pressure—opponents exploit recovery timing (~10-15f windows) to maintain Advantage:
    • Definition: Okizeme ("wakeup game") is the attacker’s strategy to pressure a prone foe as they recover, leveraging the 10f hitbox-protected startup and subsequent vulnerability. Hard knockdowns (15-20f hitstun) offer longer setup windows than soft (~5f roll), encouraging meaty attacks—hits timed to strike late in their active frames (e.g., frame 3-5 of a 5f move) for frame advantage (+5-10f on block).
    • Options:
      • Meaty Strikes: Slow-startup attacks (e.g., ~15f Heavy Attack) hit late, gaining advantage—e.g., a ~5f active window hitting on frame 4 leaves the attacker +7f if blocked, safe vs. reversals.
      • Grabs/Control Skills: Post-recovery grabs (LT/ALT) or Impact State Control Skills (e.g., Root) catch standing foes, unblockable but risky vs. jumps (~10f startup).
      • Spacing Traps: Ranged attacks (e.g., stomach-prone projectiles) or environmental hazards (e.g., collapsing debris) force recovery choices—e.g., dodge into a trap (~15f recovery).
    • Prone Counterplay: Back blocks (high/mid) or stomach ranged (+5f startup) resist pressure, but slow mobility (0.5x speed) and hitbox exposure (e.g., feet on back) limit uptime—re-knockdown risk favors attacker momentum.
    • Visual Cues: HUD tags ("MEATY," "WAKEUP") and animations (e.g., prone stagger, attack glow) signal timing—e.g., meaty hits flash "MEATY +7" on success.
    • Scalability: Okizeme shines in 1v1 but scales to group combat—e.g., multiple foes layering meaty attacks—balanced by friendly fire risks.
  • State Integration: Prone modifies movesets across states (e.g., Impact lacks overheads), retaining Energy/Poise costs as standing/crouching.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Soft Knockdown Ambiguity: Auto-stand vs. prone option risks unclear intent—e.g., does holding crouch negate the roll?
    • Solution: Define soft knockdown as ~5f roll (full invuln) with auto-stand unless crouch held by frame 3, locking prone choice—prototypable for feel (e.g., Guilty Gear throw recovery).
  • Prone Combat Strength: Full-power back blocks or stomach ranged attacks could make prone too safe—e.g., negating pressure at no cost.
    • Solution: Limit prone moves—~50% damage, ~+5f startup—offset by mobility loss (no strafing, ~0.5x speed), balancing viability vs. vulnerability; test back blocks vs. low attacks.
  • Recovery Timing: Hitbox-protected startup (10f) vs. re-knockdown (5f window) might overprotect or undervalue recovery—e.g., too easy to escape hard knockdown.
    • Solution: Mimic Guilty Gear Strive—~10f invuln on recovery startup (e.g., frames 1-10 of dodge), vulnerable post-startup; re-knockdown only in first ~5f of idle prone—balancing risk/reward.
  • Directional Landing Logic: Front/back attack direction determining stomach/back landing lacks edge-case clarity—e.g., side attacks or multi-hit moves.
    • Solution: Default side/multi-hits to stomach (ranged-ready), prioritize final hit direction—e.g., back-to-front combo lands on stomach—refining via animation testing.

Suggestions for Prototyping

  • Knockdown Timing: Test soft knockdown roll (5f invuln, auto-stand) vs. hard knockdown hitstun (15f back, ~20f stomach)—adjust for move variety (e.g., overheads = longer).
  • Prone Movesets: Trial back melee (50% damage, ~10f startup, high/mid hitbox) and stomach ranged (50% damage, ~15f startup, forward arc)—tweak vs. standing/grappling foes (e.g., "Grappling State Skills" grabs).
  • Recovery Windows: Prototype recovery invuln (10f on crouch/jump/dodge startup) and re-knockdown window (5f idle prone)—e.g., dodge roll (~15f total, invuln 1-10)—tracking okizeme success rate.
  • Okizeme Dynamics: Test meaty setups—e.g., 15f Heavy Attack hitting frame 11 (+7f on block) vs. ~10f Control Skill grab (unblockable, whiffs jumps)—and prone counters (e.g., back block vs. low meaty); measure frame advantage (+5-10f) and reset frequency.
  • Voluntary Prone: Experiment with startup (5f vs. ~10f) and drop animation—e.g., quick kneel (5f) vs. dramatic fall (~10f)—balancing tactical use vs. exposure.

Conclusion

Knockdown to Prone crafts a dynamic fall-and-rise system for The Fighting Game, blending soft knockdown’s quick recovery (5f roll, optional prone) with hard knockdown’s punishing drop (15-20f hitstun, directional landing). Prone offers a gritty, limited moveset—back melee/blocks, stomach ranged—offset by slow mobility and recovery options (hitbox-protected ~10f startup), encouraging okizeme over combos. The expanded okizeme subsystem layers meaty attacks, grabs, and traps atop prone’s vulnerability, amplifying "Core Loop"’s Advantage-to-Disadvantage shift with high-fantasy flair—downed warriors facing relentless pressure. Prototyping will refine timing, prone balance, and okizeme potency, ensuring this mechanic fuels strategic depth and cinematic tension.
