Core Loop

Introduction

The heart of The Fighting Game lies in its core loop—a fluid, cyclical system that fuses the split-second intensity of fighting games with the calculated depth of action RPGs. This loop guides players through a series of interconnected phases: Neutral, Prediction, Advantage, Disadvantage, and back to Neutral. At its core is a rock-paper-scissors dynamic where attacks overpower grabs, grabs bypass guards, and guards neutralize attacks. This creates a balanced counter-system where every action has a weakness, and every player has the potential to prevail by reading their opponent’s intentions correctly. Picture a high-fantasy gladiator duel: two warriors circle each other, blades flashing, each move a gamble, each clash a test of wits and reflexes.

The loop thrives on this interplay, ensuring that no single strategy reigns supreme. From the tense standoff of Neutral to the high-stakes commitment of Prediction, and the shifting tides of Advantage or Disadvantage, players are constantly adapting, countering, and seizing opportunities. RPG elements—gear, class abilities, and environmental interactions—layer additional depth, while the 3rd-person over-the-shoulder perspective (with intent assist and camera reticle) enhances spatial tactics. Below, each phase is broken down, spotlighting how the rock-paper-scissors mechanic shapes decisions and drives the flow of combat.

Goals

The core loop is designed with clear ambitions:

  • Genre Harmony: Merge the precision and pace of fighting games with the progression and strategy of RPGs into a seamless experience.
  • Dynamic Choices: Offer players meaningful decisions in every phase, reflecting their skill, character build, and tactical foresight.
  • Counterplay Core: Embed the rock-paper-scissors system (attacks > grabs > guards > attacks) to reward observation and punish predictability.
  • Resource Tension: Weave energy and poise management into the loop, making every action a trade-off between risk and reward.
  • Flexible Framework: Build a system that shines in 1v1 duels but scales effortlessly to group combat or future modes.
  • Arena Drama: Capture the spectacle of gladiatorial combat, where each phase evokes the thrill of outsmarting, overwhelming, or outlasting a foe.
  • Training & Onboarding: Provide clear visual tutorials, frame data in practice mode, and replay breakdowns so new players quickly grasp the RPS triangle and phase flow.

Overview

The core loop unfolds as a cycle of five phases, each feeding into the next:

  • Neutral: A standoff where players position, probe, and prepare.
  • Prediction: A decisive moment where players commit to an action—attack, grab, or guard—anticipating their opponent’s choice.
  • Advantage: The payoff for a correct Prediction, where the player seizes control.
  • Disadvantage: The cost of a misstep, where the player must endure and recover.
  • Return to Neutral: A reset to reassess and adapt for the next exchange.

The rock-paper-scissors mechanic is the engine of this cycle: attacks beat grabs by striking before the opponent can close in, grabs beat guards by exploiting a defender’s immobility, and guards beat attacks by absorbing or deflecting the blow. This triangle ensures constant tension—players must predict and counter, knowing that every move has both a strength and a vulnerability. RPG flair (like class-specific moves or gear bonuses) and the 3rd-person view (highlighting animations and arena layout) enrich this foundation, creating a loop that’s as strategic as it is visceral.

  1. Neutral

Description: Neutral is the calm before the storm—a phase where neither player holds the upper hand. It’s a tense dance of positioning and probing, like two gladiators pacing the arena sand, eyes locked, waiting for the first telltale move. Here, the rock-paper-scissors dynamic simmers beneath the surface, as players gather clues to inform their next Prediction.

  • Player Actions:
    • Movement: Dodge, strafe, or sprint to claim space or bait a reaction. A quick rogue might circle wide, while a stoic paladin stands firm.
    • Attacks: Fast jabs, heavy slashes, or projectiles test the opponent’s reflexes, costing energy and hinting at their tendencies—do they guard, dodge, or counter?
    • Cancels (Roman): cancel an attack or grab to provoke a premature guard, setting up a future counter.
    • Resource Prep: Energy regenerates steadily, while poise builds balances to a mid point, priming players for the Prediction phase.
  • Rock-Paper-Scissors Role: Neutral is the reconnaissance stage. A player might throw a light attack to see if the opponent guards (hinting at a defensive mindset) or steps forward (suggesting a grab). These subtle cues shape the next move—will you attack to catch a grab, guard to foil an attack, or grab to punish a guard?
  • RPG Integration:
    • Class and Gear: A mage’s glowing orb might probe from afar, while a warrior’s shield bash disrupts positioning. Gear like swift boots or a telescopic spear alters reach and speed.
    • Environment: Lure an opponent near a crumbling pillar or a slick oil patch, tilting Neutral in your favor.
  • 3rd-Person Impact: The camera focuses forward over the shoulder but wide enough to create an awareness of the players surroundings behind and to the side, revealing the opponent’s stance and the arena’s layout. Spot a shoulder twitch (an attack wind-up?) or a forward lean (a grab setup?), and you’ve got an edge.
  • Mindset: Neutral is about control and patience. Aggression tempts, but overcommitting risks a counter—guard against an attack you provoked, and you’ve already lost the exchange.
  1. Prediction

Description: Prediction is the heartbeat of the loop—a split-second gamble where players commit to an action based on their read of the opponent. It’s the moment a gladiator lunges, betting steel against steel, and the rock-paper-scissors mechanic takes center stage. Guess right, and you claim Advantage; guess wrong, and Disadvantage awaits.

  • Player Actions:
    • Attack: Unleash a heavy strike, combo starter, or charged spell—perfect for overpowering a grab attempt.
    • Grab: Execute a grapple, throw, or control move to seize a guarding opponent and break their stance.
    • Guard: Raise a shield, parry, or brace to deflect an attack, turning the tables with a counter if timed right.
    • Specials: Deploy class tricks—like a trapper’s snare or a monk’s stunning palm—to tilt the odds.
  • Rock-Paper-Scissors Dynamic:
    • Attacks > Grabs: A swift blade cuts down a reaching hand before it can close.
    • Grabs > Guards: A wrestler’s grip slips past a shield, hurling the defender to the ground.
    • Guards > Attacks: A parried sword leaves the attacker exposed, ripe for retaliation.
    • This interplay ensures every choice carries weight—there’s always a counter, but only if you predict it.
  • RPG Integration:
    • Resource Cost: Big attacks or grabs burn energy and poise, amplifying reward and risk. A berserker’s cleave might hit harder but leave them sluggish if it misses.
    • Class Variants: A rogue’s backstab (attack) slices through grabs, while a knight’s shield slam (grab) crushes guards. Gear—like a sticky glove or reinforced shield—tunes these options.
  • 3rd-Person Impact: Some states push the camera in closer, sharpening focus on animations. A slow wind-up screams attack, a crouch hints at guard—miss the cue, and you’re sunk.
  • Mindset: Prediction is pure adrenaline—trust your instincts, weigh the odds, and strike. It’s less about randomness and more about outthinking your foe, using every scrap of intel from Neutral.
  1. Advantage

Description: Advantage is the victor’s prize—earned when Prediction pays off, and the rock-paper-scissors gamble lands in your favor. It’s the roar of the crowd as you press the assault, chaining moves to widen your lead. Here, the counter-system keeps the opponent guessing, preventing easy escapes.

  • Player Actions:
    • Combos: String attacks into grabs or specials—like a flurry of punches into a toss—for maximum damage.
    • Pressure: Use zoning (e.g., a magical caster’s fireball barrage) or knockbacks to dictate the fight’s tempo.
    • Finishers: Spend poise on a super—like a dragon-summoning slash—to seal the exchange.
  • Rock-Paper-Scissors Role: In Advantage, mix up your actions to stay dominant. Chain an attack into a grab if they guard, or feint a grab into an attack if they dodge. The mechanic ensures they can counter—but only if they break your rhythm.
  • RPG Integration:
    • Gear Boosts: A flaming axe adds burn to attacks, while a grappling hook extends grab range. dynamic Class perks based on your moveset—like a bleed or a energy or life steal-on-hit—prolong your edge.
    • Arena Play: Slam the foe into a spiked wall or collapse a balcony overhead for bonus damage.
  • Limits: Energy dwindles with each move while making your health more vulnerable, while Psych Burst or the poise-charged Combo Break can flip the script if you overreach or become too predictable. Or if you simply have the opponent on their toes in a more guarded state they have additional options such as Guards, parries, guard attacks and shove to help them turn the tides. Push hard, but not recklessly.
  • Endgame: Drain their energy to expose health pips, then push to finish them off—every hit can builds toward victory, but greed invites reversal.
  1. Disadvantage

Description: Disadvantage is the cost of a failed Prediction—when your rock meets their paper, and suddenly you’re scrambling. It’s the gladiator on their heels, shield raised, seeking a gap to breathe, or possibly taking a beating draining you of your energy to defend yourself. The rock-paper-scissors dynamic persists, offering a lifeline if you can outlast the storm.

  • Player Actions:
    • Defense: Block to soak hits (draining energy), dodge to slip away, or parry for a risky reversal.
    • Escape: Dash, teleport, or shove to break free and reset the fight.
    • Counter: Time a guarded attack, then punish with a quick strike.
  • Rock-Paper-Scissors Role: Guards beat attacks—so block that flurry—but grabs beat guards, so watch for a switch-up. A smart read (dodging a grab, parrying an attack) can claw you back to Neutral.
  • RPG Integration:
    • Class Tools: A tank archetype's iron skin shrugs off blows, while a rogue’s smoke bomb cloud blinds the foe momentarily. Gear like a regen amulet or dodge-boosting boots buys time or creates space.
    • Resource Grit: Poise builds under pressure, fueling a super to turn the tide.
  • 3rd-Person Impact: The camera leaves you in control to track opponent’s moves—spot the end of a combo or a grab’s reach, and you’ve got your opening.
  • Mindset: Disadvantage demands resilience. Endure, adapt, and strike when they falter—survival is the first step to recovery.
  1. Return to Neutral

Description: After Advantage or Disadvantage, the loop circles back to Neutral—a fleeting pause to regroup. It’s the gladiator dusting off their armor, blade raised anew, wiser for the last clash. The rock-paper-scissors lesson lingers, sharpening the next Prediction.

  • Player Actions:
    • Recovery: Energy ticks up, poise stabilizes—pace yourself, and you’re stronger for it.
    • Reposition: Claim high ground or dodge lingering traps to reset on your terms.
    • Adapt: Did they spam attacks? Prep a guard. Favor grabs? Ready an attack.
  • RPG Integration:
    • Gear Perks: A stamina ring hastens regen, while a summoner’s minion distracts during the lull.
    • Setup: Plant a trap or buff yourself—RPG depth turns downtime into strategy.
  • Rock-Paper-Scissors Role: Reflect on the last exchange—every counter they landed is a clue for what’s next. The mechanic’s balance keeps the fight alive.
  • Mindset: Neutral isn’t a full restart—it’s evolution. Carry your scars and smarts into the next round.

Beyond 1v1

The loop scales naturally to group chaos:

  • Friendly Fire: All players can be hit by any attack, friend or foe, positioning, spacing, timing and knowledge of your options all play a role.
  • Multi-Target Moves: Sweeping attacks or explosive grabs hit clusters, guided by the 3rd-person view.
  • Focus Fire: Target a key foe (e.g., a mage) while dodging others, leaning on the counter-system.
  • Resource Stretch: More enemies tighten energy use, but the loop holds firm.

This adaptability future-proofs the design for PvE or team battles, keeping the rock-paper-scissors core intact.

The Arena’s Pulse

The loop isn’t just mechanics—it’s a story:

  • Neutral: The standoff, breath held, crowd hushed.
  • Prediction: The clash, steel sparking as bets are made.
  • Advantage: The triumph, a roar as you dominate.
  • Disadvantage: The struggle, grit against the odds.
  • Return: The reset, a new chapter in the duel.

The rock-paper-scissors heartbeat—attacks toppling grabs, grabs piercing guards, guards halting attacks—drives every phase, ensuring players always have a path to victory if they can read the moment. It’s gladiatorial combat distilled: raw, tactical, and alive with possibility.
