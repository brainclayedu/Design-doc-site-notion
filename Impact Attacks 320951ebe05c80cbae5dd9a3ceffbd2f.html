Knockdown to Prone

Goals

The Knockdown to Prone system is designed to:

  • Differentiate Knockdown Types: Distinguish soft and hard knockdowns for varied combat outcomes, enhancing tactical depth.
  • Enable Prone Dynamics: Offer a vulnerable yet viable prone state with unique combat and recovery options, enriching player choice.
  • Integrate with Core Mechanics: Tie knockdowns and prone recovery to "Core Loop"’s Disadvantage phase, rewarding opponent pressure (e.g., okizeme) and defensive resilience.
  • Reflect High-Fantasy Theme: Frame prone as a gladiatorial struggle—down but not out—mirroring The Fighting Game’s cinematic chaos.

Overview

Knockdown to Prone governs how characters fall and recover in The Fighting Game’s 3rd-person, high-fantasy battles. Most attacks trigger a soft knockdown, where the character instantly rolls backward (5f) and auto-recovers to standing—akin to a fighting game throw—unless the player holds crouch to stay prone. Specific moves (e.g., Impact State overheads) cause a hard knockdown, dropping the character to the ground with hitstun (15-20f, move-dependent), landing on their back or stomach based on attack direction (front = stomach, back = back). In prone, players wield a unique moveset: back position enables melee attacks and blocks (high/mid), stomach allows ranged attacks, with blocking flipping to back via a startup animation (5f). Prone Recovery offers multiple exits—crouch to roll into crouch, jump (tap to stand, hold to leap), or dodge to roll upright—each with hitbox-protected startup (10f invuln on key hitboxes), though hits while prone reset to soft knockdown, encouraging okizeme over combo extension. Voluntary prone entry (D-pad Down/X) has a startup (~5-10f), balancing tactical drops. This system integrates with "Impact State" (e.g., hard knockdown setups) and "Grappling State Skills" (e.g., prone grab vulnerability), amplifying "Core Loop"’s Advantage-to-Disadvantage tension with a grounded, strategic twist.

Key Features

  • Soft Knockdown: Instant backward roll (~5f invuln), auto-stands unless crouch held by frame 3 to stay prone—mimics throw recovery (e.g., Guilty Gear’s quick pop-up).
  • Hard Knockdown: Hitstun (~15-20f, varies by move) drops player prone—stomach (front attack) or back (back attack)—with HUD tag ("HARD") for clarity.
  • Prone States:
    • Back: Melee attacks/blocks (high/mid focus, e.g., kicks upward), reduced speed (~+5f startup), vulnerable at feet.
    • Stomach: Ranged attacks (e.g., projectiles), blocking flips to back (~5f startup, head invuln during flip), weak to rear/above.
  • Prone Recovery:
    • Crouch: Back rolls forward to crouch, stomach pushes up/back to crouch (~10f, hitbox-protected startup).
    • Jump: Tap stands (10f), hold leaps (15f, hitbox-protected startup).
    • Dodge: Rolls to stand (~15f, hitbox-protected startup).
    • Hits in first ~5f of idle prone reset to soft knockdown.
  • Voluntary Prone: D-pad Down/X drops to stomach (~5-10f startup), same recovery options.
  • Okizeme Subsystem: Prone state primes wakeup pressure—opponents exploit recovery timing (~10-15f windows) to maintain Advantage:
    • Definition: Okizeme ("wakeup game") is the attacker’s strategy to pressure a prone foe as they recover, leveraging the 10f hitbox-protected startup and subsequent vulnerability. Hard knockdowns (15-20f hitstun) offer longer setup windows than soft (~5f roll), encouraging meaty attacks—hits timed to strike late in their active frames (e.g., frame 3-5 of a 5f move) for frame advantage (+5-10f on block).
    • Options:
      • Meaty Strikes: Slow-startup attacks (e.g., ~15f Heavy Attack) hit late, gaining advantage—e.g., a ~5f active window hitting on frame 4 leaves the attacker +7f if blocked, safe vs. reversals.
      • Grabs/Control Skills: Post-recovery grabs (LT/ALT) or Impact State Control Skills (e.g., Root) catch standing foes, unblockable but risky vs. jumps (~10f startup).
      • Spacing Traps: Ranged attacks (e.g., stomach-prone projectiles) or environmental hazards (e.g., collapsing debris) force recovery choices—e.g., dodge into a trap (~15f recovery).
    • Prone Counterplay: Back blocks (high/mid) or stomach ranged (+5f startup) resist pressure, but slow mobility (0.5x speed) and hitbox exposure (e.g., feet on back) limit uptime—re-knockdown risk favors attacker momentum.
    • Visual Cues: HUD tags ("MEATY," "WAKEUP") and animations (e.g., prone stagger, attack glow) signal timing—e.g., meaty hits flash "MEATY +7" on success.
    • Scalability: Okizeme shines in 1v1 but scales to group combat—e.g., multiple foes layering meaty attacks—balanced by friendly fire risks.
  • State Integration: Prone modifies movesets across states (e.g., Impact lacks overheads), retaining Energy/Poise costs as standing/crouching.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Soft Knockdown Ambiguity: Auto-stand vs. prone option risks unclear intent—e.g., does holding crouch negate the roll?
    • Solution: Define soft knockdown as ~5f roll (full invuln) with auto-stand unless crouch held by frame 3, locking prone choice—prototypable for feel (e.g., Guilty Gear throw recovery).
  • Prone Combat Strength: Full-power back blocks or stomach ranged attacks could make prone too safe—e.g., negating pressure at no cost.
    • Solution: Limit prone moves—~50% damage, ~+5f startup—offset by mobility loss (no strafing, ~0.5x speed), balancing viability vs. vulnerability; test back blocks vs. low attacks.
  • Recovery Timing: Hitbox-protected startup (10f) vs. re-knockdown (5f window) might overprotect or undervalue recovery—e.g., too easy to escape hard knockdown.
    • Solution: Mimic Guilty Gear Strive—~10f invuln on recovery startup (e.g., frames 1-10 of dodge), vulnerable post-startup; re-knockdown only in first ~5f of idle prone—balancing risk/reward.
  • Directional Landing Logic: Front/back attack direction determining stomach/back landing lacks edge-case clarity—e.g., side attacks or multi-hit moves.
    • Solution: Default side/multi-hits to stomach (ranged-ready), prioritize final hit direction—e.g., back-to-front combo lands on stomach—refining via animation testing.

Suggestions for Prototyping

  • Knockdown Timing: Test soft knockdown roll (5f invuln, auto-stand) vs. hard knockdown hitstun (15f back, ~20f stomach)—adjust for move variety (e.g., overheads = longer).
  • Prone Movesets: Trial back melee (50% damage, ~10f startup, high/mid hitbox) and stomach ranged (50% damage, ~15f startup, forward arc)—tweak vs. standing/grappling foes (e.g., "Grappling State Skills" grabs).
  • Recovery Windows: Prototype recovery invuln (10f on crouch/jump/dodge startup) and re-knockdown window (5f idle prone)—e.g., dodge roll (~15f total, invuln 1-10)—tracking okizeme success rate.
  • Okizeme Dynamics: Test meaty setups—e.g., 15f Heavy Attack hitting frame 11 (+7f on block) vs. ~10f Control Skill grab (unblockable, whiffs jumps)—and prone counters (e.g., back block vs. low meaty); measure frame advantage (+5-10f) and reset frequency.
  • Voluntary Prone: Experiment with startup (5f vs. ~10f) and drop animation—e.g., quick kneel (5f) vs. dramatic fall (~10f)—balancing tactical use vs. exposure.

Conclusion

Knockdown to Prone crafts a dynamic fall-and-rise system for The Fighting Game, blending soft knockdown’s quick recovery (5f roll, optional prone) with hard knockdown’s punishing drop (15-20f hitstun, directional landing). Prone offers a gritty, limited moveset—back melee/blocks, stomach ranged—offset by slow mobility and recovery options (hitbox-protected ~10f startup), encouraging okizeme over combos. The expanded okizeme subsystem layers meaty attacks, grabs, and traps atop prone’s vulnerability, amplifying "Core Loop"’s Advantage-to-Disadvantage shift with high-fantasy flair—downed warriors facing relentless pressure. Prototyping will refine timing, prone balance, and okizeme potency, ensuring this mechanic fuels strategic depth and cinematic tension.

Knockup Attacks

Goals

The Knockup Attacks mechanic is designed to:

  • Enable Aerial Momentum: Launch foes upward from Pre-Jump and Impact States, fueling air combos in The Fighting Game’s high-fantasy skirmishes.
  • Differentiate State Roles: Contrast Pre-Jump’s jump-driven knockups with Impact’s aimed precision, enhancing tactical variety.
  • Support Mixup Play: Pair with crouch lows and Impact highs for "Core Loop"’s Prediction phase, rewarding positioning and timing.
  • Scale Customization: Allow knockup properties (e.g., height, cost) to flex with skill crafting, deepening RPG combat depth.

Overview

Knockup Attacks propel opponents into the air, exclusive to Pre-Jump and Impact States in The Fighting Game’s 3rd-person chaos. From Pre-Jump—triggered by Jump (A/Space) and Heavy Attack (RT/RMB)—the player leaps into an attack, knocking standing foes upward (20-30f airtime) and auto-jumping to pursue with aerial combos; it whiffs entirely vs. crouching foes, emphasizing high-risk, high-reward momentum. In Impact State (RT/RMB hold), Light Attack (RB/LMB) shifts to an aimed knockup, launching foes (15-20f airtime) with a distinct animation (e.g., sweeping lift)—blockable by crouching foes, no auto-jump, but an optional follow-jump (A/Space) tracks the airborne target. Both cost Energy (~5-10%, customizable), tying to "Combat Mechanics" gauges, and integrate with "Core Loop"’s Advantage phase—Pre-Jump for combo starters, Impact for controlled setups. Animations (e.g., Pre-Jump’s uppercut, Impact’s launcher) ensure readability, while crouch blocks and lows counterbalance their threat.Key Features

  • Pre-Jump Knockup: Jump + Heavy Attack launches standing foes (20-30f airtime), auto-jumps player for air combos—whiffs vs. crouching, costs Energy (5-10%, customizable), HUD "KNOCKUP."
  • Impact Knockup: Light Attack in Impact State launches standing foes (15-20f airtime), optional follow-jump (A/Space) auto-tracks foe—blockable by crouching, costs Energy (5-10%, customizable), HUD "AIRBORNE."
  • Animation Distinction: Pre-Jump uses jump-driven momentum (e.g., uppercut, high arc), Impact uses aimed precision (e.g., sweeping lift, tighter arc)—visual clarity via style and height.
  • Counterplay: Pre-Jump whiffs crouching (high property), Impact blockable by crouching—pairs with crouch lows (Base) and Impact overheads (highs).
  • State Integration: Pre-Jump ties to "Knockdown to Prone"’s soft resets, Impact aligns with knockup (low/mid) vs. knockdown (low/high)—Energy scales with customization.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Impact Knockup Orientation: Aimed knockup hitting crouching but being blocked feels odd vs. Pre-Jump’s whiff—e.g., why aim succeeds yet fails?
    • Solution: Test Impact as blockable (~+2f on crouch block) with tighter hitbox (mid-only), Pre-Jump as pure high (whiffs crouch)—refine via animation/hitbox alignment (e.g., Impact lifts mid, not low).
  • Follow-Jump Clarity: Impact’s optional follow-jump risks input overlap (e.g., A/Space vs. Base jump) or tracking confusion—e.g., does it lock perfectly?
    • Solution: Limit follow-jump to 5f startup, ~50% Energy of full jump (5%), auto-tracking within ~5m—HUD "FOLLOW" signals activation; test vs. manual jumps.
  • Airtime Balance: Pre-Jump (20-30f) vs. Impact (15-20f) airtime might blur combo potential—e.g., Impact too short for follow-ups.
    • Solution: Cap Pre-Jump at ~25f (combo-friendly), Impact at ~20f (setup-focused)—exceptions via customization (e.g., +5f height for 5% Energy); prototype combo length.
  • Crouch Dominance: Pre-Jump whiffing and Impact blockable by crouching could overvalue crouch defense—e.g., no low knockup threat.
    • Solution: Rely on Base crouch lows (5f, ~5% Energy) and Impact overheads (15f, hard knockdown)—test mixup frequency vs. knockup usage.

Suggestions for Prototyping

  • Knockup Timing: Test Pre-Jump (5f startup, ~25f airtime) vs. Impact (10f startup, ~20f airtime)—adjust for combo viability (e.g., ~3-5 air hits post-Pre-Jump).
  • Crouch Interaction: Trial Pre-Jump whiff (high hitbox, 0f on crouch) vs. Impact block (+2f on crouch block, mid hitbox)—track crouch counter rate vs. low/Impact high mixups.
  • Follow-Jump Feel: Prototype Impact follow-jump (5f startup, ~5% Energy, ~5m track range)—e.g., auto-locks within ~45° cone—vs. manual jump (10f, ~10% Energy); measure pursuit success.
  • Energy Scaling: Start at ~5% (Pre-Jump) and ~7% (Impact), tweak via customization—e.g., +5f airtime for +2% Energy—balancing spam vs. reward.
  • Animation Readability: Test Pre-Jump uppercut (5f windup, high arc) vs. Impact lift (10f windup, mid arc)—ensure HUD tags ("KNOCKUP," "AIRBORNE") sync with foe height.

Conclusion

Knockup Attacks elevate The Fighting Game’s combat with Pre-Jump’s momentum-driven launches (25f airtime, auto-jump) and Impact’s precise lifts (20f airtime, follow-jump option), fueling aerial chaos in its high-fantasy arena. Exclusive to Pre-Jump (Jump + Heavy) and Impact (Light), they contrast whiffing vs. crouching (Pre-Jump) with blockable precision (Impact), balanced by crouch lows and Impact highs in "Core Loop"’s Prediction flow. Energy costs (~5-10%, customizable) and distinct animations (uppercut vs. lift) tie them to "Combat Mechanics" and readability, while a potential "Knockback" section looms to round out directional control. Prototyping will hone airtime, crouch interplay, and follow-jump tracking, ensuring knockups shine as combo starters and setup tools.

Control Skills

Goals

The Control Skills mechanic is designed to:

  • Deliver Potent Crowd Control: Enable unblockable, RPG-inspired CC via Impact State’s Grab, amplifying opponent disruption in The Fighting Game’s high-fantasy battles.
  • Balance Risk and Reward: Pair high-impact hard CC (e.g., Stun) with steep Energy/Poise costs and diminishing returns, ensuring PVP fairness.
  • Enhance Tactical Depth: Support soft CC buildup (e.g., Slows) across attacks, chaining into hard CC for strategic combos in “Core Loop”’s Advantage phase.
  • Embrace Customization: Allow players to craft CC effects (physical or magical), mirroring Path of Exile 2’s skill diversity and fighting game versatility.

Overview

Control Skills are unblockable, high-cost crowd control attacks triggered by the Grab button (LT/ALT) in Impact State (RT/RMB hold), extending the Grab Category with RPG-style effects in The Fighting Game’s 3rd-person arena. As the pinnacle of Impact State’s “control” focus, they deliver hard CC—e.g., Stun (1-2s disable), Freeze (2s immobile)—while Impact’s Light Attack (RB/LMB) handles knockup/knockdown. Soft CC (e.g., Slows 30% speed, Knockbacks ~2-3m) builds via normals (no direction), command normals (e.g., forward + RB), or other attacks (e.g., cold-based Slow), contributing to a visible CC meter tied to Poise. Repeated Control Skills trigger diminishing returns (e.g., ~50% effect duration after 2 uses in ~10s) to prevent stunlocks, with repositioning CC (Knockback, Pull) exempt. Costs scale with effect strength (15-30% Energy, ~1-2 Poise), customizable per skill crafting. Control Skills shine in setups—e.g., Stun into Pre-Jump knockup (“Knockup Attacks”) or Pull into okizeme (“Knockdown to Prone”)—with animations (e.g., lightning Stun, wrestling Disarm) reflecting player-chosen physical or magical flair. HUD tags (e.g., “CONTROL: STUN”) and a Poise/CC meter ensure readability, balancing The Fighting Game’s chaotic PVP.Key Features

  • Impact State Control Skills: Grab (LT/ALT) in Impact State delivers hard CC—e.g., Stun (1-2s, +10f advantage), Freeze (2s), Confusion (2s input scramble), Polymorph (2s model swap), Blind (~2s vision blur)—unblockable, costs ~20-30% Energy + ~1-2 Poise, HUD “CONTROL: [EFFECT].”
  • Soft CC Buildup: Normals (e.g., RB) and command normals (e.g., forward + RB) apply soft CC—e.g., Slow (30%, ~3s), Tether (3m leash, 3s), Knockback (2-3m), Pull (2-3m), Disarm (3s no attacks), Root (2s, tentative)—costs ~10-15% Energy, builds CC meter (10-20 points per hit).
  • CC Meter: Visible Poise/CC bar (100 points) tracks buildup—hard CC triggers at ~80-100 (e.g., Slow buildup to Freeze), decays ~10 points/s after ~5s inaction. Diminishing returns (50% duration after 2 uses in ~10s, resets ~15s) limit hard CC; repositioning CC exempt.
  • Non-Control CC: Other attacks (e.g., Impact Light knockup ~20f, Grab Pull ~2m) apply soft CC, ~50% buildup contribution (e.g., ~5-10 points), weaker than Control Skills (e.g., Root ~3s vs. ~2s).
  • Input Logic: Impact Control Skills use Grab (LT/ALT), normals/command normals use Light/Heavy (RB/RT) across states—e.g., Base forward + RB = Slow, Impact back + LT = Stun.
  • Visual Distinction: Physical (e.g., wrestling Disarm) or magical (e.g., arcane Polymorph) animations, customizable—e.g., Fire Stun adds flames. HUD tags and Poise/CC bar glow signal buildup.
  • Setup Potential: Hard CC enables combos—e.g., Stun (+10f) to Pre-Jump knockup, Pull to okizeme. Soft CC sets up—e.g., Slow to Impact knockup.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Input Overlap with Grabs: Grab (LT/ALT) for Control Skills and standard grabs risks confusion—e.g., expecting grapple in Impact State.
    • Solution: Lock Control Skills to Impact State’s Grab, standard grabs to Base State—HUD tag (“CONTROL” vs. “GRAB”) and distinct visuals (e.g., magical aura vs. physical slam) clarify intent; test input recognition.
  • CC Balance: Hard CC (e.g., ~2s Stun) could stunlock without tight diminishing returns, while soft CC spam (e.g., Slows) might overwhelm.
    • Solution: Cap hard CC at 2s, diminishing returns at ~50% duration after 2 uses in ~10s—e.g., Stun ~2s, then ~1s, then ~0.5s. Soft CC limited by Energy (10-15%) and Poise/CC meter (~100 points); test PVP comeback potential.
  • Buildup Ambiguity: Visible Poise/CC meter needs clear thresholds—e.g., when does Slow trigger Freeze?—and non-Control CC’s role is vague.
    • Solution: Set 80-100 points for hard CC, ~10-20 per soft CC, ~5-10 for non-Control CC—e.g., ~5 Slows (15 points each) trigger Freeze. Meter glows at 75% threshold; prototype buildup speed vs. decay (10 points/s).
  • Effect Redundancy: Knockup, Knockdown, Knockback in Control Skills overlap with “Knockup Attacks,” “Knockdown to Prone,” and Impact Light—risking dilution.
    • Solution: Control Skill CC is stronger—e.g., Knockup 30f vs. ~20f Impact Light, Pull ~3m vs. ~2m Grab. Non-Control CC weaker (50% duration/effect); test visual/functional distinction (e.g., magical Knockback vs. physical).

Suggestions for Prototyping

  • CC Timing: Test hard CC durations—e.g., Stun 1-2s (+10f), Freeze ~2s—vs. soft CC—e.g., Slow ~30% for ~3s, Root ~2-3s. Adjust diminishing returns (50% after 2 uses in ~10s) for PVP fun (e.g., ~2-3s max disable).
  • Poise/CC Meter: Prototype visible meter (100 points)—e.g., Slow ~15 points, Impact Knockup ~10 points, hard CC at ~80-100. Test decay (10 points/s after 5s) and glow (75% threshold) for readability.
  • Resource Costs: Start at ~10-15% Energy + ~1 Poise (soft CC), ~20-30% Energy + ~1-2 Poise (hard CC)—tweak via customization (e.g., +1s Stun for +5% Energy). Measure spam vs. comeback potential.
  • Setup Chains: Test combos—e.g., Stun to Pre-Jump knockup (3-5 air hits), Pull to okizeme (15f meaty). Ensure soft CC (e.g., Slow) boosts hard CC triggers (~80 points).
  • Visuals: Trial physical (e.g., wrestling Disarm) vs. magical (e.g., lightning Stun) animations—e.g., Fire Stun adds ~5% DoT for +5% Energy. Sync HUD tags (“CONTROL: STUN”) with Poise/CC glow.

Conclusion

Control Skills anchor The Fighting Game’s crowd control, delivering unblockable, RPG-inspired hard CC (e.g., Stun, Freeze) via Impact State’s Grab (LT/ALT), complemented by soft CC buildup (e.g., Slows, Pulls) across normals and other attacks. High Energy (20-30%) and Poise (1-2) costs, paired with diminishing returns (50% duration after 2 uses), ensure PVP balance, while a Poise/CC meter (100 points) tracks buildup for strategic depth. Stronger than non-Control CC (e.g., ~30f Knockup vs. ~20f Impact Light), they fuel “Core Loop”’s Advantage phase—e.g., Stun to knockup, Pull to okizeme—with customizable physical or magical flair. Prototyping will refine durations, buildup, and visuals, cementing Control Skills as a dynamic, high-fantasy disruptor alongside grabs and knockups.
