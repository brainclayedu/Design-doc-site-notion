Knockup Attacks

Goals

The Knockup Attacks mechanic is designed to:

  • Enable Aerial Momentum: Launch foes upward from Pre-Jump and Impact States, fueling air combos in The Fighting Game’s high-fantasy skirmishes.
  • Differentiate State Roles: Contrast Pre-Jump’s jump-driven knockups with Impact’s aimed precision, enhancing tactical variety.
  • Support Mixup Play: Pair with crouch lows and Impact highs for "Core Loop"’s Prediction phase, rewarding positioning and timing.
  • Scale Customization: Allow knockup properties (e.g., height, cost) to flex with skill crafting, deepening RPG combat depth.

Overview

Knockup Attacks propel opponents into the air, exclusive to Pre-Jump and Impact States in The Fighting Game’s 3rd-person chaos. From Pre-Jump—triggered by Jump (A/Space) and Heavy Attack (RT/RMB)—the player leaps into an attack, knocking standing foes upward (20-30f airtime) and auto-jumping to pursue with aerial combos; it whiffs entirely vs. crouching foes, emphasizing high-risk, high-reward momentum. In Impact State (RT/RMB hold), Light Attack (RB/LMB) shifts to an aimed knockup, launching foes (15-20f airtime) with a distinct animation (e.g., sweeping lift)—blockable by crouching foes, no auto-jump, but an optional follow-jump (A/Space) tracks the airborne target. Both cost Energy (~5-10%, customizable), tying to "Combat Mechanics" gauges, and integrate with "Core Loop"’s Advantage phase—Pre-Jump for combo starters, Impact for controlled setups. Animations (e.g., Pre-Jump’s uppercut, Impact’s launcher) ensure readability, while crouch blocks and lows counterbalance their threat.Key Features

  • Pre-Jump Knockup: Jump + Heavy Attack launches standing foes (20-30f airtime), auto-jumps player for air combos—whiffs vs. crouching, costs Energy (5-10%, customizable), HUD "KNOCKUP."
  • Impact Knockup: Light Attack in Impact State launches standing foes (15-20f airtime), optional follow-jump (A/Space) auto-tracks foe—blockable by crouching, costs Energy (5-10%, customizable), HUD "AIRBORNE."
  • Animation Distinction: Pre-Jump uses jump-driven momentum (e.g., uppercut, high arc), Impact uses aimed precision (e.g., sweeping lift, tighter arc)—visual clarity via style and height.
  • Counterplay: Pre-Jump whiffs crouching (high property), Impact blockable by crouching—pairs with crouch lows (Base) and Impact overheads (highs).
  • State Integration: Pre-Jump ties to "Knockdown to Prone"’s soft resets, Impact aligns with knockup (low/mid) vs. knockdown (low/high)—Energy scales with customization.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Impact Knockup Orientation: Aimed knockup hitting crouching but being blocked feels odd vs. Pre-Jump’s whiff—e.g., why aim succeeds yet fails?
    • Solution: Test Impact as blockable (~+2f on crouch block) with tighter hitbox (mid-only), Pre-Jump as pure high (whiffs crouch)—refine via animation/hitbox alignment (e.g., Impact lifts mid, not low).
  • Follow-Jump Clarity: Impact’s optional follow-jump risks input overlap (e.g., A/Space vs. Base jump) or tracking confusion—e.g., does it lock perfectly?
    • Solution: Limit follow-jump to 5f startup, ~50% Energy of full jump (5%), auto-tracking within ~5m—HUD "FOLLOW" signals activation; test vs. manual jumps.
  • Airtime Balance: Pre-Jump (20-30f) vs. Impact (15-20f) airtime might blur combo potential—e.g., Impact too short for follow-ups.
    • Solution: Cap Pre-Jump at ~25f (combo-friendly), Impact at ~20f (setup-focused)—exceptions via customization (e.g., +5f height for 5% Energy); prototype combo length.
  • Crouch Dominance: Pre-Jump whiffing and Impact blockable by crouching could overvalue crouch defense—e.g., no low knockup threat.
    • Solution: Rely on Base crouch lows (5f, ~5% Energy) and Impact overheads (15f, hard knockdown)—test mixup frequency vs. knockup usage.

Suggestions for Prototyping

  • Knockup Timing: Test Pre-Jump (5f startup, ~25f airtime) vs. Impact (10f startup, ~20f airtime)—adjust for combo viability (e.g., ~3-5 air hits post-Pre-Jump).
  • Crouch Interaction: Trial Pre-Jump whiff (high hitbox, 0f on crouch) vs. Impact block (+2f on crouch block, mid hitbox)—track crouch counter rate vs. low/Impact high mixups.
  • Follow-Jump Feel: Prototype Impact follow-jump (5f startup, ~5% Energy, ~5m track range)—e.g., auto-locks within ~45° cone—vs. manual jump (10f, ~10% Energy); measure pursuit success.
  • Energy Scaling: Start at ~5% (Pre-Jump) and ~7% (Impact), tweak via customization—e.g., +5f airtime for +2% Energy—balancing spam vs. reward.
  • Animation Readability: Test Pre-Jump uppercut (5f windup, high arc) vs. Impact lift (10f windup, mid arc)—ensure HUD tags ("KNOCKUP," "AIRBORNE") sync with foe height.

Conclusion

Knockup Attacks elevate The Fighting Game’s combat with Pre-Jump’s momentum-driven launches (25f airtime, auto-jump) and Impact’s precise lifts (20f airtime, follow-jump option), fueling aerial chaos in its high-fantasy arena. Exclusive to Pre-Jump (Jump + Heavy) and Impact (Light), they contrast whiffing vs. crouching (Pre-Jump) with blockable precision (Impact), balanced by crouch lows and Impact highs in "Core Loop"’s Prediction flow. Energy costs (~5-10%, customizable) and distinct animations (uppercut vs. lift) tie them to "Combat Mechanics" and readability, while a potential "Knockback" section looms to round out directional control. Prototyping will hone airtime, crouch interplay, and follow-jump tracking, ensuring knockups shine as combo starters and setup tools.
