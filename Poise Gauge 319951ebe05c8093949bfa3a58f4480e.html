Poise Gauge

Introduction

The Poise Gauge in The Fighting Game represents a fighter’s Combat Resolve, displayed as a bar broken into five segmented blocks much like tension in Guilty Gear Xrd. It acts as a resource for powerful abilities—like Roman Cancels, Supers, and Faultless Defense—while also providing passive benefits such as reduced damage and shorter control effect durations. Starting at its midpoint (2.5 blocks), the gauge regenerates toward this point over time and shifts based on combat actions, promoting dynamic and strategic play.

Goals

The Poise Gauge aims to:

  • Enable Tactical Flexibility: Fuel Supers, Roman Cancels, and Faultless Defense with a limited, renewable resource.
  • Provide Passive Resilience: Reduce incoming damage and control effect durations based on gauge levels.
  • Encourage Active Play: Grow through offensive and defensive actions, while penalizing passivity or poor management.
  • Offer Customization: Allow gear and skills to tweak regeneration, capacity, and ability costs.

Overview

The Poise Gauge begins at 2.5 blocks (midpoint of Block 3), its natural equilibrium. It increases with actions like landing hits or blocking and decreases when using abilities or passively when above the midpoint. Passive benefits scale with its level:

  • Damage Reduction: Grows as Poise drops, enhancing durability.
  • Control Duration Reduction: Shortens stun or knockdown times, strongest at full gauge.

The Poise Guage acts as a spendable resource for:

  • Supers (3-5)
  • Faultless Guard (steady drain)
  • Guarded attacks (2-4)
  • Roman Cancels (1-3)

A Poise Break occurs when the gauge empties, halting regeneration and stripping passive benefits, making fighters more susceptible to damage and control effects.

Core Mechanics

Poise Fluctuation

  • Building Poise:
    • Landing attacks (e.g., Light Attack adds a small amount).
    • Blocking hits (e.g., blocking a Heavy Attack adds a moderate amount).
    • Parrying (e.g., a perfect parry adds a full block).
    • being hit while below 2.5 blocks (unless at 0 poise)
  • Depleting Poise:
    • Using Supers (3-5 blocks).
    • Activating Roman Cancels (1-3 blocks).
    • Engaging Faultless Defense (continuous drain).
    • Guarded attacks (2-4 blocks)
    • Being countered or parried.
    • Being hit while above 2.5 blocks
  • Passive Regeneration: Moves toward 2.5 blocks—rising if below, falling if above—unless at zero (delayed by Poise Break).

Supers

  • Costs 3-5 blocks
  • Effect: Use of the strongest attacks available to the player.

Faultless Defense

  • Cost: Initial drain plus ongoing drain while active.
  • Effect: Improves guarding but risks Poise Break if overextended.

Guarded Attacks

  • Costs 2-4 blocks
  • Effect: Interrupt an opponent from blockstun

Roman Cancels

  • Cost: 1-3 blocks (e.g., Red Roman Cancel: 1 block, Blue: 2 blocks).
  • Effect: Cancels animations for tactical resets or combo extensions, with variants (Blue, Red, Purple, Yellow) offering unique effects like slowdown or defense boosts.

Passive Benefits

  • Damage Reduction: Increases as Poise decreases—low Poise offers more protection.
  • Control Duration Reduction: Scales with Poise—full gauge greatly reduces stun or knockdown times. At max, a hit might skip hitstun, draining Poise instead.

Poise Break

  • Trigger: Gauge reaches zero, pausing regeneration and removing passive benefits.
  • Risk: Full/extended control effect durations and no damage reduction until recovery begins.

Control attacks (possibly)

  • Control attacks could also cost poise, perhaps not in blocks but as a drained value like the energy bar, limiting access to other options the same way faultless defense does.
  • Costs could scale with specials being significantly more expensive on poise.
  • This could serve as an alternative or in addition to energy drain and open up control-focused builds

Customization and Combat Dynamics

  • Gear and Skills:
    • Increase regeneration speed.
    • Reduce ability costs or expand the functionality of ability at additional cost..
  • Playstyle Impact:
    • Aggressive players build Poise quickly for cancels and Supers.
    • Defensive players leverage Faultless Defense and regeneration control.

Integration with Game Systems

  • Energy: Drives actions that build Poise.
  • Health: Low health slows regeneration, heightening tension.
  • Combat Flow: Balances offense (cancels, Supers) with defense (Faultless Defense, passive benefits).

Challenges and Solutions

  • Challenge: The midpoint regeneration (draining above 2.5 blocks) might confuse players.
    • Solution: Add tutorial hints or UI indicators (e.g., a glowing midpoint) to highlight its neutral state.
  • Challenge: Poise Break could feel too punishing, leaving players overly vulnerable at zero Poise.
    • Solution: Shorten the break delay or tie it to health—e.g., higher health reduces the penalty duration.
  • Challenge: Faultless Defense’s steady drain might deplete Poise too fast.
    • Solution: Offer gear to lower the drain rate or grant Poise boosts for successful blocks.

Additional Context

  • Environment: Areas with pressure zones boost Poise gain through increased combat.
  • Build Flexibility: Gear and skills tailor the gauge to offensive or defensive preferences.

Conclusion

The Poise Gauge links resilience to action, powering key abilities while offering passive protection. Its midpoint regeneration and Poise Break mechanics reward active engagement and tactical depth, making it a cornerstone of The Fighting Game’s combat system.
