Armor

Introduction

In The Fighting Game, Armor serves as a customizable, layered defense system that reduces incoming damage and bolsters a fighter’s durability within its high-fantasy, 3rd-person combat arena. Woven into gear and equipment, Armor protects the Energy and Health gauges, with specialized protection against High, Mid, and Low attack zones through a slot-based framework. Beyond raw damage mitigation, Armor impacts Poise, Burst, and movement, introducing strategic depth via trade-offs in mobility, resource management, and build versatility.

Goals

The Armor system aims to:

  • Reduce Damage: Lessen the impact of attacks on Energy and Health, prolonging a fighter’s staying power.
  • Enhance Zone Protection: Offer targeted defense against High, Mid, and Low attacks through distinct slot categories.
  • Balance Trade-offs: Counteract protective benefits with mobility, skill benefits or resource cost adjustments, encouraging thoughtful gear selection.
  • Support Customization: Allow intricate layering and material choices to suit varied playstyles.

Overview

Armor operates as a passive defense layer, equipped through gear across three primary categories: Head, Body, and Legs. Each category includes multiple slots aligned with the game’s attack zones:

  • Head Category (High attacks): Helmet, Coif, Head Chainmail, Neck Chain.
  • Body Category (Mid attacks): Outer Garment, Body Plate, Body Chainmail, Body Garment, Gloves, Bracers, Arm Armour.
  • Legs Category (Low attacks): Leg Chainmail, Hose/Pants, Leg Plate, Tassets, Boots.

These slots enable detailed layering—such as cloth beneath chainmail beneath plate—forming a composite defense tailored by material (e.g., cloth for blunt resistance, plate for slashing). Armor reduces overall damage to Energy and Health, with effectiveness scaling by gear weight and type. Customization through crafting or enchantments allows further specialization, such as resistance to specific damage types (e.g., fire, cold) or enhanced gameplay attributes like movement speed or Energy efficiency.

Core Mechanics

Armor Slots and Categories

The Armor system is divided into three categories, each with specific slots:

  • Head Category (High attacks):
    • Helmet slot
    • Coif slot
    • Head Chainmail slot

  • Body Category (Mid attacks):
    • Outer Garment slot
    • Body Plate slot

    • Arm Armour slot
  • Legs Category (Low attacks):
    • Leg Chainmail slot

  • Mechanics:
    • Each slot accepts a unique armor piece, contributing to damage reduction for its category’s attack zone.
    • Example: A sturdy Helmet mitigates High attack damage, while agile Gloves in the Body category preserve Energy for Mid defense.
  • Combat Role: Offers zone-specific protection—e.g., reinforced Head slots resist stuns, lightweight Legs boost mobility.
  • Tactical Use: Promotes strategic layering—e.g., fortifying the Body for mid-range clashes or balancing coverage across all zones.

Damage Reduction and Resistances

  • Mechanics:
    • Reduces Energy and Health damage by a percentage tied to gear—light armor provides modest protection, heavy armor maximizes it.
    • Slots target zone-specific damage—e.g., Head slots lessen High attack impact.
    • Materials offer resistances—e.g., cloth mitigates blunt, plate counters slashing, mail resists piercing.
    • Enchantments add elemental (e.g., fire, cold) or status (e.g., bleed) resistances.
  • Combat Role: Boosts survivability—e.g., preserving Energy against combo strings—or counters enemy tactics—e.g., fire resistance vs. pyromancers.
  • Tactical Use: Adapts defense to opponents—e.g., stacking slash resistance against blade users—balanced by gear weight.

Armor as Defense

  • Mechanics:
    • Combines slot effects into a composite resistance profile—e.g., layered cloth and plate for blunt and slashing defense.
    • Gear weight impacts Energy costs—e.g., heavy armor raises dash expenses, light armor conserves Energy (Energy Gauge).
  • Combat Role: Weighs protection against agility—e.g., heavy armor absorbs hits but slows movement, light armor favors dodging.
  • Tactical Use: Heavy builds withstand aggression, light builds emphasize speed—adjustable via slots and materials.

Alternative Defense Options

  • Evade Influence:
    • Light armor lowers Energy costs for evades, dashes, and dodges (Mobility Skills).
    • Example: Light Hose/Pants reduce dash Energy drain, supporting evasion builds.
  • Energy Shield:
    • A magical substitute for heavy armor, potentially shielding Energy or Health.
    • Example: An enchanted Coif might generate an Energy Shield, absorbing damage before Energy depletion.
  • Tactical Use: Diversifies builds—e.g., Energy Shields for spellcasters, heavy armor for brawlers—without relying on chance-based evasion.

Reinforced Armor

  • Mechanics:
    • Armor can be upgraded via crafting—e.g., armorsmith enhancements—to improve resistances or add magical traits.
    • Example: Reinforced Leg Plate resists elemental damage, reducing fire impact.
  • Combat Role: Expands Armor’s utility—e.g., cold-resistant gloves for icy arenas.
  • Tactical Use: Customizes gear to threats—e.g., reinforcing against poison for toxic foes.

Integration with Game Systems

  • Energy Gauge: Mitigates Energy loss, adjust the total energy available and modifies potential breakpoint.
  • Health Gauge: Reduces Health damage, and/or adds additional pips.
  • Poise Gauge: Improves poise skill efficiency or accelerates Poise recovery/loss under attack.
  • Mobility/Combat: Affects various timing elements such as attack speed or traversal mobility.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Challenge: Overloaded heavy slots might nullify damage.
    • Solution: Limit total reduction or tie penalties to slot count.
  • Challenge: Excessive mobility penalties from heavy gear.
    • Solution: Introduce hybrid gear or skills to offset drawbacks.
  • Challenge: Zone protection skewing balance against certain attacks.
    • Solution: Equalize slot impact—e.g., Body slots provide wider coverage—or add baseline resistances.

Additional Context

  • Environment: Slots excel in hazardous areas—e.g., reinforced Boots lessen trap damage.
  • Build Variety: Slot combinations—e.g., heavy Body for Poise, light Legs for agility—enable diverse strategies.
  • Crafting System: Armor ties to material collection—e.g., rare metals for plate upgrades—deepening progression.

Conclusion

Armor in The Fighting Game is a slot-based, customizable system—spanning Head, Body, and Legs—that delivers zone-specific protection against High, Mid, and Low attacks. Through detailed layering, material-based resistances, and trade-offs in mobility and Energy, it fosters strategic gear decisions, balancing durability, speed, and tactical flexibility for every fighter build.

Armor Types

Introduction

Armor Types in The Fighting Game categorize the diverse materials and forms of protective gear that fighters equip, each offering unique advantages and disadvantages within the high-fantasy, 3rd-person combat arena. Spanning Cloth, Leather, Metal, and Magical variants, these types range from grounded, real-world examples—like padded gambesons or steel plate—to fantastical options enabled by the game’s lore, such as dragon-scale plate or spider-silk robes. Equipped across Head, Body, and Legs slots, Armor Types provide a foundation for layered defense and customization, influencing resilience, mobility, and tactical play.

Goals

The Armor Types system is designed to:

  • Diversify Protection: Offer a range of defensive options with distinct strengths and weaknesses.
  • Enhance Fantasy Flavor: Incorporate lore-based materials (e.g., mythical beast hides) for unique properties.
  • Support Layering: Enable combinations of materials to bolster protection across slots.
  • Facilitate Crafting: Tie armor properties to material sources, enhancing player-driven customization.

Overview

Armor Types include Cloth, Leather, Metal, and Magical, each defined by material properties rather than weight tiers, equipping slots like Helmet (Head), Body Plate (Body), or Boots (Legs). Cloth offers padded layers (e.g., gambesons) or magical robes, Leather provides mid-tier organic options (e.g., brigandine), Metal delivers robust defense (e.g., chainmail), and Magical enhances gear with runes or exotic materials (e.g., dragon scales). These types can be layered—e.g., a cloth gambeson beneath metal plate—to create composite armor, balancing physical and magical resistances with weight and mobility trade-offs.

Core Mechanics

Cloth

  • Description: Encompasses fabric-based armor, equipping slots like Coif (Head), Body Garment (Body), or Hose/Pants (Legs).
  • Subtypes:
    • Gambeson:
      • Padded wool layers, affordable and common—e.g., Body Garment slot.
      • Strong against blunt attacks, weak to stabbing, mildly resistant to cutting; used alone or under mail/plate.
      • Variants include noble padded jackets for added charisma and protection.
    • Pourpoint/Jupon:
      • Lighter quilted jackets—e.g., Outer Garment slot over plate.
      • Offers minor protection, often ornamental, enhancing layered defenses.
    • Robes/Cloaks:
      • Magical-focused—robes as holistic armor, cloaks layered over gear—e.g., Body Garment or Coif slots.
      • May provide Energy Shields or runic protections for low-armor builds.
      • Could provide alternative benefits catered towards affecting attacks and their effect potency
    • Clothing:
      • Casual underlayers—e.g., Hose/Pants slot.
      • Minimal defense with potential cloth-like benefits (e.g., slight blunt resistance).
  • Combat Role: Balances low protection with mobility or magical utility—e.g., robes supporting Energy-focused play (Energy Gauge).
  • Tactical Use: Lightweight base—e.g., gambeson under chain—or standalone magic defense—e.g., cloaks with runes.

Leather

  • Description: Organic armor, equipping slots like Gloves (Body) or Leggings (Legs), with magical imbuement potential.
  • Subtypes:
    • Leather Gambeson:
      • Cloth-leather hybrid—e.g., Body Garment slot.
      • Heavier than cloth, adds slashing/piercing resistance to blunt defense.
    • Leather Armor:
      • Lightweight, tight-fitting—e.g., Arm Armour slot.
      • Balanced slashing/piercing protection, retains mobility for agile builds.
    • Leather Lamellar:
      • Small linked plates—e.g., Bracers slot.
      • Weak to piercing (gaps), good mobility; pairs with mail or gambeson for torso/shoulder coverage.
    • Brigandine:
      • Cloth/leather with riveted steel—e.g., Body Plate slot.
      • Stronger slashing/piercing resistance, transitions to metal armor.
  • Combat Role: Mid-tier protection—e.g., leather armor aids Energy efficiency (Energy Gauge)—with organic flexibility.
  • Tactical Use: Agile defense—e.g., lamellar for speed—or hybrid strength—e.g., brigandine for durability.

Metal

  • Description: Heavy, durable armor for slots like Head Chainmail (Head), Body Plate (Body), or Leg Plate (Legs).
  • Subtypes:
    • Chainmail:
      • Interlocking rings—e.g., Leg Chainmail or Neck Chain slots.
      • Good vs. piercing, decent vs. slashing, weak to blunt; pairs with gambeson for blunt coverage.
    • Scale:
      • Overlapping scales—e.g., Tassets slot.
      • Vulnerable to piercing, decent slashing defense; less protective than plate.
    • Plate:
      • Expensive, rigid plating—e.g., Body Plate or Leg Plate slots.
      • Near-impervious to slashing/piercing, weak to blunt; best with gambeson/mail for gaps.
  • Combat Role: Maximizes physical defense—e.g., plate boosts Poise regen when hit (Poise Gauge)—at mobility’s cost.
  • Tactical Use: Tanks hits—e.g., chainmail vs. spears—with layered support—e.g., plate over gambeson.

Magical

  • Mechanics: Enhanced via runes (sewn/engraved), magical materials (e.g., dragon scales, spider silk), or jewel sockets—e.g., Helmet or Body Garment slots.
  • Properties: Adds resistances (e.g., fire, cold) or effects—e.g., Energy Shield recharge (Energy Gauge).
  • Combat Role: Counters exotic threats—e.g., scale plate resists fire—or enhances utility—e.g., silk robes boost Energy.
  • Tactical Use: Expands builds—e.g., runed gauntlets for elemental defense—via crafting.

Integration with Game Systems

  • Energy Gauge: Cloth/Leather reduce Energy costs (e.g., dashes)—Metal increases them—e.g., plate raises sprint cost (Page 17).
  • Health Pip Gauge: All types mitigate Health pip loss—e.g., brigandine softens Mid attack damage (Page 18).
  • Poise Gauge: Metal aids Faultless Defense—e.g., reduced drain—Cloth boosts regen—e.g., robes speed Poise recovery (Page 23).
  • Combat Flow: Balances agility (e.g., leather) and endurance (e.g., plate) via material choice (Combat sections).

Challenges and Solutions

  • Challenge: Plate’s slashing/piercing resistance might dominate—e.g., fights skewing defensive.
    • Solution: Scale weight penalties—e.g., slower movement—or limit stacking—e.g., fewer plate slots.
  • Challenge: Cloth’s low defense could falter—e.g., weak vs. piercing foes.
    • Solution: Enhance magical perks—e.g., runes add resistance—or layering benefits—e.g., gambeson + mail synergy.
  • Challenge: Material complexity might overwhelm—e.g., tracking layered resistances.
    • Solution: Simplified UI—e.g., slot icons show strengths—or crafting guides—e.g., “+Blunt Resistance.”

Additional Context

  • Crafting: Fantasy materials—dragon scales, spider silk—unlock unique perks—e.g., scale plate resists fire (Page 24).
  • Environment: Armor adapts—e.g., leather lamellar for caves, plate for open fields—enhancing arena play.
  • Build Variety: Material-slot combos—e.g., cloth coif + metal plate—offer flexible defense strategies.

Conclusion

Armor Types in The Fighting Game—Cloth, Leather, Metal, and Magical—forge a material-driven defense system, enriched by fantasy lore and crafting potential. From gambesons resisting blunt trauma to runed robes shielding with magic, each type offers distinct protection across Head, Body, and Legs slots. Integrated with Energy, Health, and Poise, this system rewards strategic material choices and layering, anchoring fighters’ resilience in every clash.
