Controls

Controls

Introduction

The control system is the lifeline of The Fighting Game, channeling player intent into a vibrant tapestry of high-fantasy combat through a 3rd-person over-the-shoulder lens. Forgoing hard lock-on targeting, it merges fighting game precision with action RPG versatility, delivering a responsive experience optimized for gamepads and robust on keyboard/mouse. From swift directional strikes to precise skillshots and commanding specials, the controls orchestrate movement, combat states, and strategic depth—tailored for intense 1v1 duels with scalability for group battles. Every input is a gladiator’s flourish, bridging the arena’s chaos to the player’s command.

Goals

The system aims to:

  • Blend Genres Seamlessly: Fuse fighting game sharpness with RPG adaptability, supporting combos, specials, and class-driven mechanics.
  • Clarify State Roles: Ensure smooth transitions between directional and aimed states, each intuitive and distinct.
  • Balance Inputs: Prioritize gamepad ergonomics with keyboard/mouse parity for broad appeal.
  • Enhance Camera Flow: Pair controls with a dynamic, soft-lock camera for immersion and awareness.
  • Refine Execution: Provide motion inputs with buffered accessibility and skill-based depth.
  • Scale Forward: Build a foundation for duels that adapts to group combat or summon-based play.

Overview

The scheme revolves around a Base State—the default for movement and core actions—and three Held States (Impact, Guard, Focus), triggered by holding bumper/trigger buttons to modify attacks and unlock aimed specials. Charged attacks are available across all states, with directional or aimed behavior configurable via skill/moveset assignment. The 3rd-person camera shifts: wide and offset in Base State for awareness, tightening in Held States with a soft-lock reticle for precision. Special moves use motion inputs with negative-edge execution (hold, move, release) and a 5-10 frame input buffer, categorized as aggressive, stalwart, or evasive. Face buttons maintain consistent movement actions, while bumpers/triggers drive combat variety, keeping thumbsticks free for aiming.

Key Mechanics

Base State

Active with no buttons held, offering directional movement and combat actions via left thumbstick (or WASD) relative to the camera:

  • Light Attack: Fast directional strike (e.g., jab); hold for a brief period for charged variant, release alone for stronger hit (directional by default, configurable).
  • Heavy Attack: Stronger directional blow; hold for a brief period for charged variant, release alone for powerful strike (directional by default, configurable), or press RB/LT for Held State actions.
  • Guard: Blocks attacks; holding enters Guard State.
  • Grab: Proximity control moves; holding enters Focus State.
  • Dash/Dodge/Evade: Unified repositioning (tap for dodge, hold for dash).
  • Jump: Leaps upward, height scaling with hold; holding enters Pre-Jump State.
  • Sprint: Boosts movement speed.
  • Crouch: Enters Crouch State for low attacks or stealth.
  • Prone: Enters Prone State voluntarily or via knockdown, limiting actions defensively.
  • Combo Attack Y: Situational combo or crowd-control move.
  • Combo Attack X: Additional situational strike or state-specific effect.
  • Super: High-impact, poise-costing attack (RB + RT + LB) with freeze effect.
  • Burst: Resource-driven disruption (RB + LB + LT) for escape or reset.
  • Camera: Wide, offset left (like Assassin’s Creed Valhalla), with attack-triggered snapping and charge glow for visual feedback.
  • Charged Variants: Light, Heavy and Grab attacks can all be charged. Because these enter states charged attacks from the base state only come out if other state-based options are not otherwise activated. (ie, charging a heavy attack enters the impact state but if you release the button before inputting impact state options a changed heavy attack can come out)
  • Directional Variation: Because the base state is Directional but the player still faces forward in this state non forward directional attacks are limited by visibility and so are restricted to close range.

Pre-Jump State

  • Activation: Hold A/Spacebar.
  • Purpose: Enables Impact-style knockup attacks.
  • Actions:
    • Light Attack: Directional knockup (e.g., rising jab); hold RB (for a brief period), release for charged knockup (directional by default, configurable).
    • Heavy Attack: Stronger directional knockup (e.g., leaping smash); hold RT (for a brief period), release for charged variant (directional by default, configurable).
  • Exit: Release A/Spacebar triggers the jump, returning to Base State.

Held States

Holding bumper/trigger buttons modifies attacks into aimed variants and unlocks specials, with charged options configurable:

  1. Impact State (Hold RT/RMB)
    • Activation: Heavy Attack hold; overridden by RB/LT press for actions, else charged attack on release.
    • Purpose: targeted/aimed disruption—knock downs, knock ups, and Control Attacks (e.g., stuns, roots).
    • Modified Actions:
      • Light Attack: Impact heavy; Aimed knockup or overhead smash; hold RB (15-20 frames), release for charged variant (aimed by default, configurable).
      • Dash/Dodge/Evade: Wild Assault; Rush in for an unexpected assault
      • Grab: Control Skill; Limit your opponent's actions for a brief respite or to open them up for an assault.
      • Motion Inputs: provide powerful variations on these actions.
  1. Guard State (Hold LB/CTRL)
    • Activation: Guard hold.
    • Purpose: Defensive options—parries, deflects, counters.
    • Modified Actions:
      • Block: Sustained guard; timed release for parry/reflect.
      • Light Attack: guarded attack; interrupts an attacking opponent and reset the momentum of the match.
      • Heavy Attack: Deflect Shield; pushing opponents away and opening them up for a punish.
      • Motion Inputs: provide powerful variations on these actions.
  1. Focus State (Hold LT/ALT)
    • Activation: Grab hold.
    • Purpose: Multi-target/AoE or precision skillshots.
    • Modified Actions:
      • Light Attack: Skillshots (e.g., energy bolt); hold RB, release for charged/special variant.
      • Heavy Attack: AOE or aimed strike (e.g., fireball); hold RT, release for charged/special variant.
      • Motion Inputs: Specials like an AoE blast or piercing shot.
  • Camera: Tightens over-the-shoulder (like God of War 2018), with strong soft-lock and fast orientation smoothing.

Motion Inputs

Specials use motion inputs with negative-edge execution and a 5-10 frame input buffer, or charge by holding:

  • Aggressive: Ends forward (e.g., quarter-circle forward + release RT).
  • Stalwart: Ends backward (e.g., back-forth + release LB).
  • Evasive: Ends sideways (e.g., side-to-side + release LT).
  • Charged: Held for a minimum duration with scaling effects for longer hold duration. This may replace motion options.

Modifier States

  • Crouch State (Hold/Press RTS/C): Low attacks, reduced hitbox.
  • Prone State (Press D-pad Down/P or Knockdown): Limited actions, defensive benefits.

Charged Attacks

  • Activation: Hold RB/LMB (Light) or RT/RMB (Heavy) for 15-20 frames in any state (Base, Pre-Jump, Impact, Guard, Focus).
  • Behavior: Release alone triggers a charged attack; directional in Base/Pre-Jump (e.g., charged jab), aimed in Held States (e.g., charged Impact smash). Pressing another button during hold (e.g., RB in RT hold) shifts to Held State actions, canceling the charge if the held key is overwritten by another attack (making it the state key); otherwise, the charge carries over. Configurable via skill/moveset (directional or aimed).
  • Feedback: Glow or wind-up animation signals charge.

Input Priority

Resolves input conflicts:

  • Supers/Burst Override All: RB + RT + LB (Super) and RB + LB + LT (Burst) execute over all states/actions within a 5-frame window, avoiding option selects.
  • Guard State Over Held States: LB/CTRL trumps Impact (RT/RMB) and Focus (LT/ALT) unless Supers/Burst trigger.
  • Held States Over Base Attacks: Impact and Focus modify Light, Heavy, Grab when held, unless charged attack releases alone.
  • First-Held Priority: Among Impact and Focus, first held button prevails.
  • Movement Persistent: Dash/Dodge/Evade, Jump (with Pre-Jump), Sprint, Crouch, Prone execute universally.
  • Combo Attacks Contextual: Y/Q and X/E adapt to active state, secondary to Held State entry.

Scalability

  • Group Combat: Directional attacks sweep foes; Impact disables singles, Focus hits multiples.
  • Summons/Puppets: Soft-lock and smoothing adapt without lock-on limits.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Input Fatigue: Holding RT/LT/LB risks strain.
    • Solution: Optional toggle (e.g., double-tap RT) for Held States.
  • Motion Input Timing: Negative-edge could slow response.
    • Solution: 5-10 frame buffer eases execution, preserving pacing.
  • No Lock-On: Risks disorientation with summons/groups.
    • Solution: Strong soft-lock and fast smoothing ensure clarity, avoiding lock-on.
  • Input Density: Many Base State actions.
    • Solution: Unified Dash/Dodge/Evade; Jump distinct with Pre-Jump; tight chords for Supers/Burst.
  • State Overlap: Impact/Focus roles might blur.
    • Solution: Impact for single-target control, Focus for multi-target/AoE, inputs on bumpers/triggers.
  • Attack spaces in aimed States: An attack that is aimed upwards or downwards may create a disconnect visually or confuse the player depending on how this is handled. (e.g., Aiming upwards at an opponents head and throwing out a mid/low attack may feel bad or confusing to the player as the attack space does not line up with the aimed space expectation.)
    • Solution: To Be Determined.

Conclusion

The controls fuse fighting game finesse with RPG breadth—intuitive, deep, and scalable. With Supers/Burst overriding all, charged attacks across states with configurable directionality, and a Pre-Jump twist, it delivers a fantasy combat experience that’s both accessible and masterful.
