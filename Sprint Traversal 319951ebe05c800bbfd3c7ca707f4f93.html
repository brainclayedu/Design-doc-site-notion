Sprint/Traversal

Sprint/Traversal

Sprinting and traversal enhance movement in The Fighting Game, weaving speed and environmental mastery into its high-fantasy, 3rd-person combat experience. Sprinting propels players across the arena with rapid bursts of momentum, while traversal leverages an approach inspired by Brink’s S.M.A.R.T. (Smooth Movement Across Random Terrain) system—allowing intuitive, fluid navigation over obstacles like walls, ledges, and gaps. Both mechanics are governed by Energy costs and shaped by character builds, ensuring they complement the game’s tactical pacing and enrich combat without overshadowing its core rhythm.

Goals

The sprinting and traversal system is designed to:

  • Balance Speed and Control: Provide swift movement options that enhance positioning without disrupting combat spacing, keeping sprinting tactical rather than constant.
  • Elevate Arena Interaction: Transform environments into interactive spaces, rewarding clever traversal with strategic advantages.
  • Support Build Diversity: Adjust sprint and traversal performance through stats, gear weight, and class skills, reflecting player customization.
  • Preserve Combat Focus: Position sprinting and traversal as secondary to core combat movement, maintaining deliberate, skill-driven engagement.

Overview

Sprinting accelerates ground movement for closing gaps or evading threats, draining Energy continuously—more so under attack—and introducing momentum that shifts players into a Sprinting State with unique, high-impact attacks. Traversal, inspired by Brink’s S.M.A.R.T. system, predicts player intent to seamlessly vault over obstacles, climb walls, or wall-run along ledges, adapting to the terrain without complex inputs. This approach turns arenas into dynamic, multi-level battlegrounds, where agile fighters sprint and traverse with ease, while heavier builds trade speed for durability, making every move a reflection of tactical intent and player design.

Core Mechanics

Sprinting

Sprinting significantly boosts speed, ideal for covering distance or pursuing foes, drawing inspiration from momentum-driven dashes but tempered for combat balance.

  • Mechanics: Drains Energy over time, with increased cost when under attack—light builds sustain it longer, heavy ones tire quickly. Momentum lingers after stopping, transitioning into a Skid State with reduced control, while agility enhances speed and strength mitigates knockback.
  • Combat Role: Enters a Sprinting State with momentum-charged attacks—like a ramming strike or sliding kick—offering unique offensive options. Blocking during sprinting may carry a recovery penalty, prioritizing offense over defense.
  • Tactical Use: Closes wide gaps—such as chasing a retreating opponent—or flees hazards, balanced by the risk of overshooting or exposure during the skid.

Skid State

The Skid State follows sprinting, a brief slide where momentum delays stopping, akin to sliding mechanics in fast-paced shooters but with tactical consequences.

  • Mechanics: Maintains forward motion briefly, preventing instant blocks—agility shortens the skid, strength reduces stumble risks. Costs no additional Energy beyond sprinting.
  • Combat Role: Limits immediate defense, encouraging follow-up dashes or attacks—like a sliding tackle—to maintain momentum.
  • Tactical Use: Extends sprint range but exposes players to counters, requiring foresight to avoid overextension.

Slide State

Crouching mid-sprint triggers a Slide State, a low-profile skid that dodges or engages, blending parkour fluidity with combat utility.

  • Mechanics: Reduces the hitbox and costs a small burst of Energy—light and heavy build traveling differently depending on the momentum they carry. Transitions smoothly from sprinting, ends in the crouched state.
  • Combat Role: Enables low attacks—like a sweeping kick—or evasion of high strikes, offering versatility mid-motion.
  • Tactical Use: Ducks under projectiles or sets up ground counters, balanced by its brief duration and Energy draw.

Traversal

Traversal employs a S.M.A.R.T.-inspired approach—Smooth Movement Across Random Terrain—predicting player intent to navigate obstacles fluidly, much like Brink’s system but tailored for combat integration.

  • Mechanics: Costs Energy per action—Hurddling less than vaulting less than clambering. S.M.A.R.T. pre-analyzes terrain for reachability (e.g., ledges, walls), auto-executing simple vaults and requiring manual inputs (jump, grab) for complex moves like climbing or wall-running, blending intuition with control.
  • Combat Role: Supports limited attacks mid-traversal—enhancing surprise or positioning—without dominating combat, keeping the focus on core engagement.
  • Tactical Use: Scales heights for ambushes—e.g., climbing to drop on foes—or bypasses obstacles like gaps or barriers, balanced by Energy costs and exposure risks.

Traversal State Attack Variations

Traversal state attacks turn movement into offense, offering dynamic strikes mid-action inspired by Brink’s seamless parkour-combat blend. These are conceptual possibilities, balanced by Energy costs and timing risks.

  • Vaulting Attacks:
    • Concept: Strikes mid-vault, such as a kick or weapon swing as you leap over obstacles.
    • Role: Disrupts foes below or sets up follow-ups—e.g., kicking an enemy to interrupt their stance.
  • Climbing Attacks:
    • Concept: Attacks from walls or ledges, like a downward stab or thrown object.
    • Role: Targets enemies below or harasses from height—e.g., striking a foe from a ledge perch.
  • Swinging Attacks:
    • Concept: Strikes mid-swing from ropes or hooks, such as a sweeping slash or targeted kick.
    • Role: Hits foes dynamically—e.g., slashing across a group during a swing.

These variations encourage players to merge traversal with combat, enhancing tactical flexibility while maintaining strategic depth.

Integration with Combat and Builds

Sprinting and traversal integrate with the game’s broader systems:

  • Energy Dynamics: Sprinting drains Energy continuously, traversal uses bursts—light builds conserve more, heavy ones expend faster—tying to the Energy gauge. Attacks in these states add costs, preserving balance.
  • Build Influence:
    • Agility: Boosts sprint speed and traversal fluidity—e.g., faster wall-runs.
    • Strength: Reduces knockback or Energy drain—e.g., stable sprints under attack.
    • Gear Weight: Light armor enhances speed; heavy armor bolsters resilience.
  • Combat Ties: Sprinting flows into dashes or jumps, traversal links to aerial strikes—e.g., vaulting into a dive—or prone slides, ensuring seamless transitions.

Additional Traversal Elements

The following are potential ideas to expand traversal, inspired by Brink’s S.M.A.R.T. system and prior mechanics:

  • Grappling Hooks: Swinging or pulling to ledges with an Energy cost, enhancing mobility—e.g., crossing gaps effortlessly.
  • Wall-Jumping: Bouncing between walls for vertical ascent, requiring agility—e.g., scaling tight spaces.
  • Ceiling Clings: Brief hanging for stealth or ambushes, tied to Energy—e.g., dropping from above.
  • Interactive Hazards: Triggering traps via traversal—e.g., vaulting to activate a hazard—adding tactical depth.
  • Dynamic Cover: Shifting obstacles mid-sprint—e.g., toppling debris for defense—offering strategic options.

These additions could enhance traversal’s role through class skills or arena design, balanced by Energy demands.

Challenges and Solutions

Sprinting and traversal present design considerations:

  • Sprinting Overuse: Excessive sprinting might disrupt combat pacing.
    • Solution: High Energy drain for heavy builds and momentum delays (Skid State) keep it situational.
  • Traversal Complexity: Parkour could overwhelm or feel clunky.
    • Solution: Leverage S.M.A.R.T.’s predictive navigation for simple actions, reserve manual inputs for precision, and utilize energy costs to balance and limit frequency.
  • Build Imbalance: Agile builds might dominate mobility.
    • Solution: Strength perks—e.g., knockback resistance during sprints—ensure heavy builds compete.

Conclusion

Sprinting and traversal, with traversal inspired by Brink’s S.M.A.R.T. system, elevate movement into a tactical cornerstone of The Fighting Game. Sprinting offers rapid dashes with momentum-driven strikes, while traversal—vaulting, climbing, swinging—turns arenas into vibrant stages of strategy and skill. Enhanced by attack variations and potential expansions, rooted in Energy, stats, and build diversity, these mechanics ensure every run or leap amplifies combat, rewarding players with a fluid, expressive experience.
