Introduction

The Core Combat Systems are the heart of the game, defining how every attack, defense, movement, and resource decision comes together in a deep, strategic combat experience. This hub page serves as the central overview, connecting the major pillars of combat — Core Systems, Movement & Camera, Controls & Resources, Defense, Attacks & Offense, Equipment and, Environments — and showing how they support the five state categories (Targeting, Combat, Motion, Traversal, and Camera-Tilt) and the overall Yomi loop. Together, these categories create a modular foundation that scales from tight 1v1 mirror matches to large-scale MOBA and MMORPG combat without major rework.

Goals

  • Deliver a tight, strategic Yomi loop that rewards prediction, execution, and adaptation.
  • Create meaningful combat states and movement enabling players to access a large amount of combat options without cluttering their controls with individual commands.
  • Build constant resource tension through layered gauges that drive constant and interesting decision-making.
  • Ensure the 3rd-person perspective feels cinematic while remaining readable, deliberate, and fair (God of War distance with intent assist).
  • Ensure the system is modular and scales cleanly from 1v1 to MOBA and MMORPG
  • Facilitate smooth transitions between actions and states in a 3rd-person context.
  • Harness RPG elements like gear, weapons, and abilities to custom-tailor combat approaches.
  • Keep controls intuitive while supporting high skill expression and customization.
  • Key Differentiators: No hotbar → state overlaps | Layered resources → constant tension | Gear shapes states → personalized Yomi

Overview

Combat revolves around a state-based Yomi cycle: Neutral positioning → Prediction (commit to attack, grab, or guard) → Advantage/Disadvantage resolution → Return to Neutral. Resources (layered Health/Energy/Burst Pips + Points) create perpetual trade-offs, while five state categories that work together. Only one state can be active per category at a time, but they overlap to produce the final attack or skill, unlocking contextual options via motion inputs and gamepad bumpers/triggers. No skill hotbar — everything flows from core controls, customized per character archetype. Movement serves as a foundational mechanic for positioning, evasion, and setup, supporting the core loop without directly participating in its commitments.

Equipment

Gear that allows players to customize and enhance their combat capabilities. Equipment includes weapons, armor, and accessories that directly influence attack properties, resource costs, state transitions, and available movesets. Armor provides damage mitigation, poise (resistance to impact and stagger states), and bolsters guard and defensive transitions, while weight class introduces key trade-offs: lighter armor enables faster movement, sprinting, traversal fluidity, and evasive dodging at the cost of reduced protection, whereas heavier armor increases stability, raw power, and stagger resistance but slows state transitions and raises energy demands during aggressive actions. These choices fundamentally shape how players navigate the state categories and engage the Yomi loop, enabling highly personalized fighting styles.

Scaling Vision

The Core Combat Systems are built for seamless expansion across all phases of development:

  • Core Systems (States, Combat States, Core Loop) form the foundation that scales from 1v1 duels to team coordination and large-scale PvP without rework.
  • Movement & Camera expand from precise third-person control in arenas to traversal across massive persistent worlds and team-based positioning.
  • Controls & Resources grow from individual resource management to shared gauges and team resource strategies in MOBA and MMORPG play.
  • Defense evolves from personal blocking and parrying to group defense, peeling, and area control.
  • Attacks & Offense scale from personal combos and contextual mechanics to team synergy, AoE skills, and large-scale offense.
  • Weapons & Environments transition from individual gear customization to systemic economy, environmental hazards, and world-altering interactions.

Summary

The Core Combat Systems create a deep, satisfying combat loop that feels like Guilty Gear mindgames in a God of War body. Every category is designed to work together modularly, providing a strong 1v1 foundation today while remaining ready to scale into team-based MOBA play and full systemic MMORPG combat tomorrow — all while staying fun, fair, and true to the original vision.
