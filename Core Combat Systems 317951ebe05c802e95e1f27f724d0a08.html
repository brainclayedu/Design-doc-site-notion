Core Combat Systems

Overview

This section presents the complete combat and movement foundation of the fighting game. All mechanics are built around a strong Yomi loop with meaningful resource tension and cinematic physical chaos in a readable 3rd-person perspective. The systems are intentionally designed as a scalable foundation: every mechanic is created with future expansion in mind (MOBA team play, MMORPG large-scale battles) while remaining complete and satisfying for pure 1v1 and small-group duels. The focus is on player expression through states, resources, and customization rather than rigid numbers or fixed timings.

Goals

  • Deliver a tight, strategic Yomi loop that rewards prediction, execution, and adaptation.
  • Create meaningful combat states that enable players to access a large amount of combat options without cluttering their controls with individual commands.
  • Establish resource tension that drives constant and interesting decision-making.
  • Ensure the 3rd-person perspective feels cinematic while remaining readable, deliberate, and fair.
  • Build a modular foundation that scales naturally into MOBA and MMORPG experiences without major rework.
  • Facilitate smooth transitions between actions and states in a 3rd-person context.
  • Harness RPG elements like gear, weapons, and abilities to custom-tailor combat approaches.

Core Loop

The core loop unfolds as a cycle of five phases, each feeding into the next:

  • Neutral: A standoff where players position, probe, and prepare.
  • Prediction: A decisive moment where players commit to an action—attack, grab, or guard—anticipating their opponent’s choice.
  • Advantage: The payoff for a correct Prediction, where the player seizes control.
  • Disadvantage: The cost of a misstep, where the player must endure and recover.
  • Return to Neutral: A reset to reassess and adapt for the next exchange.

This loop is supported by state-based movement, resource management, and crowd control that rewards skillful play and creative customization.

Main Combat States

The game is built around distinct, meaningful states that change how movement, attacks, and defense function:

  • Base State: Default, directional combat with balanced mobility.
  • Impact State: Aimed precision mode for knockups, knockdowns, and powerful Control Skills.
  • Guard State: Blocking and defensive posture with mid/low variants and pushback.
  • Control State (Grab/Grappling & Crowd Control): Unblockable proximity attacks leading to grappling or high-impact CC effects.
  • Sprint/Traverse State: Enhanced mobility options that tie into traversal and positioning.

Resources & Gauges

Resources create constant tension and decision-making throughout every fight. The system emphasizes trade-offs between aggression, defense, and setup.

Customization Integration

Character building begins here as the primary way players define their playstyle and control space. Modular skills and new character introductions allow players to shape how they influence the fight.
