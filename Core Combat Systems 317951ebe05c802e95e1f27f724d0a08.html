Core Combat Systems

Introduction

The Core Combat Systems form the beating heart of the fighting game prototype — a 2-character mirror-match MVP designed to prove a high-depth Yomi loop in a cinematic, over-the-shoulder 3rd-person perspective. Drawing from posture tension, readable action flow, and contextual combat depth, this system delivers layered decision-making without control clutter. Every element is modular for solo UE5.7 Blueprint implementation (Mover 2.0 for states), flexible for playtesting, and scales seamlessly to MOBA 5v5 and full MMORPG without rework. MVP focus: tight 1v1 duels in a single arena, no hotbar, intent-assist camera with environment dissolve.

Goals

  • Deliver a tight, strategic Yomi loop that rewards prediction, execution, and adaptation.
  • Create meaningful combat states that enable players to access a large amount of combat options without cluttering their controls with individual commands.
  • Establish resource tension that drives constant and interesting decision-making.
  • Ensure the 3rd-person perspective feels cinematic while remaining readable, deliberate, and fair (God of War distance with intent assist).
  • Build a modular foundation that scales naturally into MOBA and MMORPG experiences without major rework.
  • Facilitate smooth transitions between actions and states in a 3rd-person context.
  • Harness RPG elements like gear, weapons, and abilities to custom-tailor combat approaches.

Overview

Combat revolves around a state-based Yomi cycle: Neutral positioning → Prediction (commit to attack, grab, or guard) → Advantage/Disadvantage resolution → Return to Neutral. Resources (layered Health/Energy/Burst Pips + Points) create perpetual trade-offs, while states (Base/Impact/Guard/Control/Sprint) unlock contextual options via motion inputs and gamepad bumpers/triggers. No skill hotbar — everything flows from core controls, customized per character archetype. Movement serves as a foundational mechanic for positioning, evasion, and setup, supporting the core loop without directly participating in its commitments.

Core Mechanics

Combat Triangle (Core RPS Foundation)

See Core Loop subpage for the full diagram and phase breakdown:
The core loop unfolds as a cycle of five phases, each feeding into the next:

  • Neutral: A standoff where players position, probe, and prepare.
  • Prediction: A decisive moment where players commit to an action—attack, grab, or guard—anticipating their opponent’s choice.
  • Advantage: The payoff for a correct Prediction, where the player seizes control.
  • Disadvantage: The cost of a misstep, where the player must endure and recover.
  • Return to Neutral: A reset to reassess and adapt for the next exchange.

The rock-paper-scissors dynamic operates within Prediction and its resolutions: attacks overpower grabs, grabs bypass guards, guards neutralize attacks. This creates balanced counterplay focused on engagement reads.

Movement as Core Mechanic

Movement underpins neutral play and transitions across all phases. It includes walking, sprinting, jumping, crouching, traversal, and quick bursts (dash/dodge/evade).

  • Dash/Dodge/Evade functions as a neutral spacing tool: it allows players to avoid attacks or grabs, reset positioning, bait commitments, or disengage without entering a direct Prediction commitment.
  • These tools cost Energy (scaled by build/gear) and integrate with states (e.g., faster in Base, limited in Impact). They support footsies and mindgames by manipulating space and timing, but do not form part of the core RPS triangle.

Gauges & Resources (Pips Visualization)

Layered "pipes" for readability in 3rd-person (embed your diagrams here):

  • Health Pips: Remaining (teal) → Shadow → Grey (recoverable) → Lost (pink).
  • Energy: Usable (cyan) → Grey (recoverable) → Lost. Buffers Health; refunds on perfect defense.
  • Burst: Purple bar for clutch reversals (Psych Burst, Wild Assault).
    Points resource for supers/Deflect Shield. VFX: Flashing recoverable segments; UI customizable for context (e.g., "Shove available").

States & Movement Integration

  • Base: Directional attacks/mobility (primary home for dash/dodge/evade).
  • Impact: Aimed CC (knockups).
  • Guard: Mid/low block + pushback.
  • Control: Unblockable grabs/grapples.
  • Sprint: Traverse/positioning.
    Transitions via Mover 2.0 (deterministic, Blueprint state machine).

Integration with Other Systems

  • Customization: Weapons/Armor alter state transitions and movement (e.g., heavy armor extends Guard pushback; agile gear boosts dash distance).
  • Camera: Locks over-shoulder; auto-adjusts for states (zoom on Impact; track movement intent).
  • Movement: Mover 2.0 handles state blends; ties to Gauges (low Energy slows Sprint/dash).
  • Subpages: [Controls], [Movement], [Camera], [Gauges], [Attacks], etc. — all plug into this core.

Playtest & Iteration Notes

  • MVP Metrics: Win rates in 2-char mirror; state usage heatmaps; dash/dodge frequency in Neutral vs. Prediction phases; recoverable resource pickup rate.
  • Flex Points: Frame data, recovery timers, pip counts, dash invuln windows — all exposed in Blueprints for rapid iteration.
  • Early Tests: Confirm movement supports but does not overshadow core RPS (target: balanced engagement frequency). Target: 50/50 RPS baseline, 70% player retention after 10 matches.
