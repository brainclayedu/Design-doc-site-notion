Cancels

Cancels

There are many types of cancels. A cancel is the act of interrupting the current action and starting a different one.

While most cancels are available on hit/block, some are available on whiff, and a rare few are even only available on block!

Special Cancel

Most normal attacks can cancel into specials and Supers. This is generally used to create combos and blockstrings, but can have other applications as well.

Note that while most normals can be special canceled even during recovery frames, normal light attacks can only be special canceled during active frames ( normals can still Gatling into other normals during recovery frames).

Jump Cancel

Some normal attacks can cancel into jump and super jump. This is used in combos as well as pressure.

Air attacks can only be jump canceled when the character has the air option available

Projectile normals can be jump canceled once the projectile touches the opponent.

Prejump Cancel

See Prejump for more details.

Dash Cancel

Some attacks can cancel into a unique ground dash with a fixed duration that varies by character (see character's frame data for more info).

Some airborne attacks can cancel into forward air dash.

animation cancels

Most normals (and command Normals) can animation cancel into other normals (And command Normals) bypassing the recovery animation and flowing immediately into the start of the next attack, this is known as an animation cancel, or cancel for short. These combos allow players to string attacks together in a way that bypasses part of the animation of an attack making them come out faster.

These combo strings are restricted by rules that determine what type of attacks can come after the previous one in order to continue to bypass the recovery animation. These rules have yet to be fully determined but the overall goal will be to ensure this feels logical and fluid, Allows for variety and expression but maintains a clear function for the use of Roman cancels to expand and extend the cancel system.

The Core influence here is Based on the Gatling system of Guilty Gear Strive. This system allows for canceling normals of the same weight into each other and then also into Command Normals with a few additional elements based on the attack types available. The transition into longer combo strings that mix attack types then gets supported by the Roman Cancel. This allows players to force combinations by canceling at a cost.

However; unlike GGST this game is a 3d fighter from an unusual over-the-shoulder perspective, as a result, we anticipate movement to be more common than not. It is far more likely that command normals would be one of the most common attack types and there is not much variety in the attack type space with the base being light attack and heavy attack.

Considering how combo breaking is setup to function it would make the most sense for lights and heavy’s to cancel into each other in some ways otherwise combo breaking becomes much easier to determine. Separating the states may make the most sense:

Requiring a Roman cancel to transition safely from base state, to heavy/dust state and slash state with charged states/specials acting in place of the traditional command normal that connects them all.

Alternatively Restricting access to heavies from lights except into dust would be another approach, this would allow easier combo breaking. Either a Poise cost could be added to the combo breaker or it could be as an opportunity for opponents to create combo break traps since the attack type would be more observable.

A third option could be similar to the last but allow upward movement of some sort, with no “mid” attack type this would have to be light to heavy as the dominant route perhaps with a restriction in going back the other way while still allowing multiple light or heavy attacks to cancel into other light or heavy as long as you aren’t moving from heavy to light. This could still maintain the cancels to specials and then supers flow.

At the moment this third option feels the most in line with the game's goals.

Cancels do not need to be done immediately, players often delay their attacks to act as a frame trap - baiting overeager opponents to retaliate, then hitting them out of the start-up of their attacks. Note that while most normals can cancel even during recovery frames, jumping can only cancel during active or pre-jump frames.

Not all attacks cancel into each other however some moves can still be strung (linked) together into a combo if the opponent’s hitstun or blockstun is still active when the player's attack is finished it’s recovery.

A player's class/build may influence possible cancel options in various ways to create greater variety in builds. It may be possible to both expand cancelable options or restrict them to gain some other benefit. As such a more restrictive set of initial cancel options may be appropriate as the default. On the other hand, a common issue with these systems is that it makes new characters/classes/builds more difficult to learn and also makes opponents harder to learn. This is a common issue with some fighting games, that said the customizable nature of this game may make knowledge of your opponent harder to read. The character-building elements and the way movesets and classes are created should all work towards maintaining a consistent logic to help make the opponent's “kit” more predictable to some extent.

Perhaps the best way to approach this would be to include how Roman cancels can be modified by the players build as this is a key to enabling extending combo strings.

Roman Cancels

The Roman Cancel system in Guilty Gear -Strive- is designed to be multifaceted and comes in various colors. Each color of Roman Cancel releases a shockwave in a circle around your character with different properties and uses.

Dash Input Roman Cancel

Dash in a cardinal direction just before Roman Cancel (excluding Yellow Roman Cancel)

Sike, I wasn't actually there!

Also referred to as "Drift Roman Cancel". Moves the character up/down/left/right during Roman Cancel's super freeze.

  • Available for any Roman Cancel except Yellow.
  • Can only drift up, down, left or right. Diagonal inputs are ignored.
    • Direction + Dash macro or Direction ×2 both work. Neutral + Dash macro is a forwards drift.
  • Red Roman Cancel shockwave will launch the opponent differently on hit depending on the dash input chosen.
    • The opponent launches up and away if you don't drift, lower and much farther away if you drift forward, higher and much closer if you drift backwards, much lower and closer if you drift downward, and much higher and closer if you drift upward.
    • Launch duration on a standing opponent: Up (68F) > Back (62F) > Neutral (55F) > Forward (48F) > Down (42F).
Cancel the Roman Cancel

Input an attack during Roman Cancel's super freeze (excluding Yellow Roman Cancel)

Go straight into the next attack of your choice!

Also referred to as "Fast Roman Cancel" or "Quick Roman Cancel". It is referred to as "Roman Cancel Cancel" in the game tutorials.

  • Available for any Roman Cancel except Yellow.
  • Inputting a move during a specific window of the Roman Cancel's super freeze (when the initial effects from a Roman Cancel disappear but before the shockwave occurs) cancels the super freeze directly into the inputted move.
    • Poise rapidly drains during super freeze at a rate of 2% per frame. By canceling the Roman Cancel, players can retain retain some Poise. The Roman Cancel will spend between 36% and 48% Poise depending on how soon it is canceled.
  • Still applies a reduced amount of slowdown at close range, even without the shockwave.
  • Preserves momentum from before the Roman Cancel, including from Dash Input Roman Cancel.
  • Combined with Dash Input Roman Cancel to move slightly in a direction before canceling.
Yellow Roman Cancel

Roman Cancel during blockstun

Turn the tables with this powerful defensive option!

Yellow Roman Cancel is used to stop an attacker and reset the momentum of the match. Upon activation, the defender creates a shockwave, granting the defender massive frame advantage. Yellow Roman Cancel is a useful tool for challenging an opponent’s pressure, especially for characters that are lacking in character-specific tools.

However, since YRC is -16 on block and leaves the defender in Counter Hit state, astute attackers can block YRC on prediction and punish it heavily. Practice utilizing all of defensive options available so as to not end up over relying on Yellow Roman Cancel and burning Poise too quickly.

  • On hit, Yellow Roman Cancel slows down the opponent.
    • In the extremely rare case that a Yellow Roman Cancel is combo'd into, it will launch the opponent instead.
  • On block, Yellow Roman Cancel is punishable and leaves the user in Counter Hit state during their recovery.
  • Hits opponents when invincible.
    • Hits based on a proximity check to an opponent's collision box, similar to a grabs. This means it cannot hit extended hurtboxes from long range attacks.

Kara Cancel

A Kara cancel is canceling the startup of a move into another move. This name comes from the Japanese word 空 (kara), which means empty or sky. You are canceling the startup of the first move such that it never comes out (and thus empty). Only certain moves can be kara canceled (usually normal attacks), and what they can be canceled into varies (usually specials, supers).

Normals and grounded dash cancels can kara cancel into special moves, Supers, Faultless Defense, Deflect Shield or Psych Burst during the beginning of their start-up.

Special moves can be kara canceled into Supers during the beginning of their start-up.

Kara cancels are a way to make inputs a bit more lenient. For example, a player mistiming chorded attacks so as to start with a non chorded input would then cancel into the chorded input. The window to kara cancel these moves usually isn't very long (usually 2-3 frames).

At this point you may wonder why would you want to do this? Competitive players have found more advanced applications for this by finding specific moves with certain properties that would benefit another move
