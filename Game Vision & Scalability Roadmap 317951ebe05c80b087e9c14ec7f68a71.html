Game Vision & Scalability Roadmap

1. Core Vision

The Fighting Game is the beginning of a larger, interconnected ecosystem of high-fantasy action games built around one deeply refined combat system.At its heart, this is a 3rd-person arena fighter that blends the precision, Yomi, and skill expression of traditional fighting games with rich RPG customization and high-fantasy cinematic combat. The game emphasizes meaningful choices in every moment: resource management, state-based movement (Base, Impact, Focus), crowd control buildup, and skill crafting that lets players create truly unique playstyles.The long-term ambition is not just one game, but a nested family of experiences that evolve from the same core DNA:

  • Fighting Game (the foundation)
  • MOBA (the expansion)
  • MMORPG (the final evolution)

Each game is a complete, standalone product with its own identity and monetization potential, but they are deliberately designed to build upon one another. Progress, knowledge, and mechanics learned in one directly enhance the next. Revenue from earlier stages can fund the development of later ones.

2. The Nested Relationship

Each game is built as a complete, standalone product, but the combat system is intentionally designed as a shared foundation that evolves and expands across all three, with the fighting game serving as the prototype and proving ground for all future mechanics.

The three games exist as distinct yet interconnected layers:

  • Fighting Game (Core)
    The pure 1v1 (and small-group) experience. This is where the combat system is born, tested, and perfected. All foundational mechanics — movement states, resource tension, skill customization, camera readability, and Yomi — are designed and balanced here first.
  • MOBA Expansion
    The combat system scales to team-based 5v5 arena play. New systems (minions, towers, objectives, larger-scale positioning) are built directly on top of the fighting game foundation. This stage lets us test group combat, friendly fire, minion interactions, and larger arena dynamics in a controlled environment.
  • MMORPG Expansion
    The world opens into a persistent, living universe. The same combat DNA expands into open-world PvP/PvE, guilds, large-scale battles, crafting economies, and long-term progression. Everything from the fighting game and MOBA feeds into this final layer.

The relationship is evolutionary, not additive. Each game stands on its own, but together they create a powerful through-line: players who master the fighting game will feel at home in the MOBA and powerful in the MMORPG.

3. Design Philosophy & Constraints

  • Start Narrow, Think Broad:
    The fighting game must feel complete and satisfying on its own. However, every major system is intentionally designed with future scaling in mind (e.g., no hard lock-on, support for multi-opponent chaos, modular skill system, strong netcode foundation).
  • Combat as the Through-Line:
    The core combat feel must remain recognizable and satisfying across all three games, even as new layers (team play, persistent world, large-scale destruction) are added.
  • Revenue & Sustainability:
    Each game is built to generate revenue that can fund the next stage. The fighting game can be premium or live-service, the MOBA can use battle passes/cosmetics, and the MMORPG can expand into subscriptions, cosmetics, and player-driven economies.
  • Room to Pivot:
    The upper layers remain flexible. We will not force MOBA or MMORPG elements into the fighting game, but the foundation will be ready when the time comes.

4. Target Audience & Tone

  • Primary Audience:
    Fighting game players who want deeper RPG expression, RPG players who enjoy skill-based combat, and high-fantasy fans who love cinematic action.
  • Tone:
    Epic, strategic, empowering, and cinematic. Every duel should feel like a story moment, and every build should feel uniquely yours.

5. Success Metrics (by Stage)

  • Fighting Game:
    Strong 1v1 community, positive reviews on combat feel and customization, sustainable player base.
  • MOBA:
    Healthy team matchmaking, competitive scene, positive feedback on how the combat scales.
  • MMORPG:
    Engaged persistent world, meaningful guild play, long-term retention.
