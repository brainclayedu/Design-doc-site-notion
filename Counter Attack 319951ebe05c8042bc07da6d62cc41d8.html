Counter Attack

Counter Attack

Goals

The counter attack system aims to:

  • Reward Precision Timing: Enhance attacks that interrupt opponent actions for greater impact.
  • Facilitate Reaction Windows: Provide hitstop and slowdown for strategic response opportunities.
  • Amplify Visual Feedback: Deliver clear, dramatic cues for successful counters across types.
  • Integrate with Combat Flow: Balance counter hit effects within existing attack mechanics.

Overview

Counter Attack in The Fighting Game designates a Counter Hit as an attack that strikes an opponent before their attack’s recovery phase ends or during specific vulnerable recovery states, boosting damage and applying type-specific hitstop and slowdown effects to enhance readability and follow-up potential. With four Counter Hit types—Very Small, Small, Mid, and Large—this system escalates from subtle boosts to cinematic pauses, affecting both players and a surrounding radius, while Mid and Large types clear the input register for a tactical reset. Excluding Grabs and Control Skills as Punishes and Supers as immune, Counter Hits refine combat precision in both 1v1 duels and group scenarios, integrating seamlessly with the game’s mechanics.

Core Counter Attack System

Counter Attacks operate as follows:

  • Mechanics: Triggered by hitting an opponent during their attack’s active frames before recovery begins or during vulnerable recovery states—such as whiffed Grabs, Projectiles, non-attacking Specials, or DPs (dragon punches)—a Counter Hit increases the attack’s damage and applies type-specific effects; hitstop pauses both players (and potentially a radius) for reaction time, followed by local slowdown doubling the receiver’s frame duration in a rapid ramp that slows then accelerates back to normal—Mid and Large Counter Hits clear the input register, resetting queued actions for combo or defensive shifts.
  • Types: Every Strike and Projectile attack has a Counter Hit type—Very Small, Small, Mid, and Large—determining properties:
    • Very Small: Base hitstop (attack-specific), no slowdown, no additional advantage—common for Projectiles and follow-up Specials.
    • Small: Base hitstop, 11 frames of immediate local slowdown, +6 frame advantage—rarely used.
    • Mid: 21 frames of hitstop overriding base values, 25 frames of local slowdown post-hitstop, +13 frame advantage, clears input register—typical for Strikes.
    • Large: 31 frames of hitstop overriding base values, 35 frames of local slowdown post-hitstop, +18 frame advantage, clears input register, camera zoom-in—strong Strikes.
    • Grabs (Base state) and Control Skills (Impact state) cannot Counter Hit, registering as Punishes instead; Supers are immune to Counter Hits, even in startup—visual and audio cues escalate with type, from subtle to dramatic.
  • States: Available across all states (Base, Focus, Impact, Guard) on ground or in air via Jump-ins—only Strikes and Projectiles trigger Counter Hits, with defaults to weapon-aligned attacks (e.g., longsword Slash) in Base or state normals (e.g., Light attack) in Aimed states when unspecified; local slowdown affects the opponent and a surrounding radius, enhancing group combat readability.

Integration with Combat

Counter Attacks integrate with other combat systems to enhance gameplay:

  • Attack Types: Strikes (light/heavy) and Projectiles (e.g., Base Forward + Light) across states trigger Counter Hits with type-specific effects—Grabs (Base Grab button) and Control Skills (Impact Grab button) register as Punishes, not Counter Hits, while Supers remain immune, aligning with "Attack Types" definitions; command normals like a Focus Forward + Heavy Strike can Counter Hit based on their type.
  • Controls: Counter Hits utilize state-specific inputs—Base thumbstick for directional Strikes, Focus/Impact/Guard via Held State inputs (e.g., Impact Light button for Heavy Strikes)—air Counter Hits extend to Jump-ins, leveraging timing across all states’ air movesets without Grab or Control Skill eligibility.
  • Attack Properties: Counter Hits boost damage without altering base properties like Cold or Bleed—e.g., a Base Heavy Strike Counter Hit increases damage output, with skills enhancing effects like Poison independently, maintaining clarity across "Attack Properties."
  • Attack Levels: Counter Hits amplify damage atop hitstun (e.g., Lv.2 = 16f)—hitstop pauses both players (e.g., Mid 21f), followed by slowdown (25f) and additional frame advantage (+13f) for the attacker, balanced via prototyping against "Attack Levels" startup speeds; hitstun applies post-hitstop to the receiver only, distinct from the universal pause.
  • Attack and damage Priority: Strikes and Projectiles as Counter Hits compete with opponent Strikes—Grabs and Control Skills punish without Counter Hit status, while Supers resist interruption; local slowdown radius aids multi-opponent control, with friendly fire risks potentially slowing allies or assists, reinforcing spacing dynamics.
  • Normal Attacks: Counter Hits extend across air and ground movesets—e.g., a Base Light Strike or Focus Forward + Light Projectile can Counter Hit, with Mid/Large clearing inputs for resets—vulnerable recovery states (e.g., whiffed Projectiles) align with state mechanics.
  • Jump In: Counter Hits enhance Deep Jump-ins—e.g., a late-hit Base Strike Counter Hit on wakeup boosts damage and frame advantage, amplifying okizeme pressure across states without Grab or Control Skill involvement.
  • Core Loop: Counter Hits probe Neutral by punishing active or whiffed attacks, secure Advantage with amplified damage and frame advantage for follow-ups, and shift Prediction by resetting inputs—group combat scales via slowdown radius, impacting multiple foes with potential ally effects.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Counter Hit Eligibility: Excluding Control Skills as Punishes alongside Grabs requires clarity against "Attack Types" Control priority or "Normal Attacks" Impact mechanics, risking ambiguity.

    Solution: Confirm Control Skills (Impact Grab button) as Punishes, not Counter Hits—prototyping will test Strikes and Projectiles against Control Skill recovery (e.g., Impact Control Skill whiff), refining "Normal Attacks" and "Attack Types" if Control Skill Counter Hit exclusion needs adjustment for consistency.

  • Frame Integration: Separating hitstop (e.g., Mid 21f pause for both) from hitstun (e.g., 16f receiver-only) and slowdown (25f) with additional frame advantage (+13f) may disrupt pacing—hitstop length needs smoothing.

    Solution: Define hitstop as a universal pause, followed by hitstun and slowdown for the receiver—prototyping will test hitstop duration (e.g., reducing Mid from 21f to 15f) to smooth gameplay feel, ensuring reaction windows remain effective without stalling flow.

  • Slowdown Radius: Local slowdown as a rapid ramp (e.g., Mid 25f) affects opponent and radius—undefined range risks slowing allies or assists per "Attack and damage Priority" friendly fire, impacting group combat readability.

    Solution: Set radius to opponent vicinity (e.g., ~2m)—prototyping will test slowdown’s multi-opponent utility and ally impact, adjusting range or excluding allies if friendly fire disrupts assist synergy, balancing readability with group dynamics.

  • Group Combat Condition: Counter Hits require the opponent’s attack to target the player in group scenarios—multi-opponent complexity may misassign counters otherwise.

    Solution: Implement a check ensuring the countered attack would hit the player—prototyping will validate this in group combat, refining detection to prevent false Counter Hits from stray attacks, aligning with "Attack and damage Priority" intent.

Conclusions

Counter Attacks enhance combat precision by rewarding strikes and projectiles that interrupt opponent actions—before recovery or during vulnerable states like whiffed Grabs—with increased damage and escalating effects from Very Small to Large types, including hitstop pauses, rapid local slowdown ramps, and frame advantage across all states. Integrating seamlessly with "Attack Types," "Control Skills," "Attack Properties," "Attack Levels," "Attack and damage Priority," "Normal Attacks," and "Jump In," they exclude Grabs and Control Skill as Punishes and Supers as immune, scaling effectively for group combat through slowdown radius while managing friendly fire risks. Prototyping will refine eligibility, frame balance, radius impact, and group combat conditions, ensuring Counter Hits amplify the core loop’s strategic flow with clear, rewarding precision.
